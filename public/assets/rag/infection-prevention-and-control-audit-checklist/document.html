THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
INFECTION PREVENTION & CONTROL AUDIT CHECKLIST

Introduction
The purpose of this document is to provide the practice with a framework with which to manage and monitor the practice for infection control purposes and CQC compliance (Regulation 15 re: premises, as well as the registration requirement that practices are charged with "assessing the risk of, and preventing, detecting and controlling the spread of, infections, including those that are health care associated").
It may be considered an Infection Prevention & Control Risk Assessment and should be retained for inspection when required.
See also: Infection Control Policy [*] and Premises Standards (*)
(IPC = Infection Prevention and Control)
Coronavirus Updates from 2022
Updated IPC advice published on 17 January 2022 says that 'an FFP3 respirator (or equivalent), must be worn by staff when...caring for patients with a suspected or confirmed infection spread by the airborne route (during the infectious period)'.
Where a risk assessment indicates it, RPE should be available to all relevant staff. The risk assessment should include evaluation of the ventilation in the area, operational capacity, and prevalence of infection/new SARS-CoV-2 variants of concern in the local area. The hierarchy of controls can be used to inform the risk assessment. Staff should be provided with training on correct use.
Practices must have undertaken the necessary risk assessments and arranged for staff to have been fit tested before ordering the equipment. It is a legal requirement on employers that all tight fitting RPE, such as, FFP3 masks must be fit tested on all health and care staff who may be required to wear one to ensure an adequate seal/fit according to the manufacturer's guidance.
The updated (form January 2022) guidance can be found here;

The remaining sections of this assessment checklist are divided into issues for general and administrative areas of the practice, then into specific areas and rooms where IPC standards dictate special attention should be given. It is recommended that each clinical and/or treatment room in the practice area should be individually assessed against the standards set by the Dept. of Health IPC guidance document dated July 2015. Special notice should be taken of guidance available on the CQC Website and especially GP Practice "Mythbusters" which cover many IPC and premises-related issues.
This checklist is not exhaustive but covers the main areas for IPC compliance in most Primary care settings and should also provide sufficient information for the annual IPC Audit.
General IPC Standards - cleanliness & cleaning Yes No Comments/action required
Cleaning
Is there a daily and weekly cleaning specification written down for each area/room?
Does any cleaning contractor have a service level agreement and is it monitored?
Is the specification reviewed on a regular basis and is evidence of the review available?
Are mops/buckets colour-coded for use in specific situations/rooms?
Are mops etc. stored clean and dry?
Is there a `dirty` or `sluice` room for the safe handling of cleaning water (emptying mop buckets etc.) with sink at a low level?
Is there evidence that cleaning staff are IPC-trained?
Do cleaning staff have access to appropriate protective clothing/equipment and cleaning materials?
Are room curtains and blinds cleaned as part of the specification?
Are modesty curtains / blinds/ sheets in clinical areas disposable or washable?
If washable - are laundry procedures IPC compliant? (i.e. washable at 71OC for 3 minutes or 65OC for 10 minutes.)
Is there a system for ensuring regular cleaning behind radiators/ air ventilation systems/pipe work and dust-traps, e.g strip light diffusers?
Waste and Contaminated Waste Yes No Comments/action required
Is there a waste and clinical waste policy, regularly reviewed?
Is there a weekly waste and clinical waste disposal contract in operation with a service level agreement available?
Are waste consignment notes provided at each collection and are they available for inspection?
Are non-clinical and clinical waste being mixed in the same waste bins?
Are external waste bins / skips cleanable and locked to prevent interference?
Is clinical waste awaiting collection securely locked away, and the room/bin hazard-marked?
Are all sharps bins and clinical waste bags sealed when put for collection and do they bear the date of sealing, and by whom?
Are clinical waste sharps bins correctly colour-coded to indicate contents (e.g. purple for contents that are cytotoxic?)
Are sharps bins more than 2/3rds full or not properly click-sealed before being put for collection?
If the practice shares a building with another waste producer - is the origin of all waste being collected known and separately identifiable?
(NB Waste Registration requires the producer to always know what is in all waste that they present for collection)

General operational hygiene and cross-contamination issues Yes No Comments/action required
Are there written instructions (with review dates) available relating to:
Hand Hygiene for staff
Use and location of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
Bodily fluid spillage and the handling of specimens inc. those in by patients
Sharps and Needle Stick Injury
Decontamination of equipment, inc. sterilisation if appropriate or procedures for use and disposal of single-use instruments
If the practice sterilises its own equipment, is there a documented procedure and equipment that complies with the Medical Devices Agency publication "Benchtop Steam Sterilisers - Guidance on Purchase, Operation and Maintenance" and Medical Devices Directive (93/42/EEC)?
Is antibacterial gel/foam available in all general areas and at access/exit points (staff & patient)?
Are toys in waiting areas etc. wipe-clean and regularly cleaned by a designated person?
Is all general seating impervious to spillage and wipe-clean? Is it clean and stain-free?
Is all general seating intact, without any tearing or other break in the fabric?
Are floors in general areas vinyl/non-slip and clean, or if carpeted are they regularly cleaned?
Is there a clean entry mat to the premises?
Are all walls in public areas and clinical/admin areas impervious and wipe-clean or painted with a wipe-clean paint?
Are walls clean and free of gouges etc. that can harbour dust/bacteria?
If there are pictures and/or notice boards on the walls are they wipe-clean and regularly dusted/replaced if necessary?
Is the building free from clutter, particularly in Reception/admin areas and waiting rooms?
Are all flat surfaces wipe-clean and dust-free and free from clutter?
Are all door, cupboard and drawer handles and stair rails visibly clean?
Is there a procedure and location available to segregate patients who may attend suffering from infectious diseases (eg: measles/ chicken pox, D&V etc)
If the practice has an automatic BP Machine for patients to self-test, does it have a cleaning and disinfecting schedule and someone nominated to regularly monitor its cleanliness?
Has the practice risk-assessed for Legionnaires Disease and implemented any actions arising?
Is there a dedicated vaccine fridge with evidence of temperature monitoring?

Toilets (staff and public) Yes No Comments/action required
Does every toilet facility have a hand-wash basin without overflow and plug?
Does every toilet facility have hot water available, and hands-free operated taps?
Does every toilet facility have liquid soap (i.e. no soap bars) and antibacterial gel/foam?
Does every toilet facility have either a wall-mounted dispenser for paper towels and/or a wall-mounted air-dryer?
Does every toilet facility have pedal-operated or automatic waste bins, with a disposable liner?
Does every female toilet facility have sanitary bins and a system in place to safely remove and replace them regularly?
Is there sufficient ventilation or air extraction - and is any ducting etc. visibly clean
Is there an adequate supply of toilet paper available where required?
Are staff and patient/public toilet areas separate (as preferable)?
Is there a toilet facility, properly equipped, for the use of disabled patients and staff?
Is there a baby-changing facility available, even if not within a washroom facility?
Does the baby-changing facility have somewhere to safely dispose of soiled nappies etc. and is wipe-clean, with a supply of antibacterial spray, a hand-wash basin and paper towels available?
Does the baby facility have instructions to users regarding its use and cleanliness?
Are all hand-wash basins throughout the practice premises only used for that purpose, visibly clean and are they sealed to the wall behind and with wipe-clean splashbacks?
Kitchen/ food consumption area(s) Yes No Comments/action required
Is the kitchen clean and furniture, and are its fittings in good repair and wipe-clean, with a wipe-clean floor and work surfaces?
Is the sink clean, without a plug or overflow, and have liquid soap/paper towels available?
Is there a designated refrigerator(s) for food only and is it regularly cleaned and monitored, and emptied daily of unused/out of date/unwrapped food?
Does the kitchen have a hands-free lined bin for all waste food etc?
Is the rest /dining room clean and furniture and fittings in good repair and wipe-clean?
Are all carpets clean and in good repair?
If patient samples are stored for collection, is the receptacle clearly marked and secure?
If patient samples have to be kept cool, is the refrigerator used ONLY for that purpose?
Are there documented procedures for safely dealing with patient samples that may be visibly contaminated or presented in inappropriate containers?
