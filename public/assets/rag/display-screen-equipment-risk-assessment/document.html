This Risk Assessment checklist is in two parts.

Part A - RISK ASSESSMENT CHECKLIST: WORKSTATION

A workstation risk assessment checklist should be completed for every desk/workstation at which an operator sits/stands to use a PC or any other equipment with a display screen (collectively referred to as Display Screen Equipment [DSE]).

Instructions for completing the checklist: If the Actual meets the Ideal, tick the Actual box; if not, put details of the standards found in the Actual box. Assess any risks arising out of the standards found and enter a risk rating; i.e. high, medium or low in the risk rating box. Detail the remedial action needed, relevant to the risk rating, and enter the date once all action is completed.

5 Document holder and screen side by side, and directly in front of user when facing desk squarely.

User looking down slightly to see the screen.

Screen positioned to minimise reflection from overhead lights and glare from windows. Move the screen and document holder in front of the user and roughly in line with keyboard.

Adjust the distance of the screen from the user's eyes without altering the user's position. Try to achieve the optimum of around 500mm.

Adjust the height of the screen so that the user is looking down 20° - 40° from the horizontal to the centre of the screen.

Ensure screen is not positioned with a window directly behind or in front.

Adjust the position and angle of the screen so that, looking from the user's eye position, reflection from overhead lights is minimised. If glare/reflection cannot be eliminated or moved/reduced to a level/position where it is not causing the user difficulty and if it is considered a medium/high risk, provide an anti-glare screen.
