THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
FIRST AID POLICY

Introduction

The First Aid at Work Guidelines for Employers, from the Health and Safety Executive, are effective as of October 1 2009, and are supplemental to the Health & Safety (First Aid) Regulations 1981. A new type of first aid needs-assessment will identify what type of first aid training your first aiders need, how many first aiders you should have (if any) and where they should be located.

Employers have a responsibility to ensure that workers can receive attention if they become ill or are injured at work, regardless of whether this is a work-induced condition or not. Equipment, trained staff, or facilities are therefore to be proportionate in relation to the risk, which in turn is appropriate to each individual workplace.

The purpose of this policy document is to set out the practice policy in relation to first aid provision to ensure the health and safety of staff. There is no statutory requirement for visitors' or patients' first aid, however practices are recommended to consider these groups within their provisions. It is designed to cover instances of minor injury or illness and (subject to appropriate training) the provision of a first response to a medical condition.

Legislation and Provisions

The Health & Safety (First Aid) Regulations 1981, as updated and effective from October 2009, require adequate first aid provision for staff who may become ill or are injured at work. This may reasonably be extended to include visitors to the premises, and it may also be extended to cover provision for staff working outside the main premises (e.g. on home visits).

For those who are self-employed (Partners), the legislation requirement to make adequate first aid provision still applies. Self-employed partners, typically sharing premises with staff, visiting clinicians and others are required to provide adequate first aid provision for themselves, following a suitable risk assessment based on their activities and workplace needs.

Legislation requires, as a minimum, a first aid box and an appointed person to oversee first aid provision. An appointed person is not necessarily a trained first aider, but rather a person responsible for the provision of the first aid equipment / stock and for overseeing the facility in the workplace, including training and alerting the emergency services if needed. They may, or may not render first aid.

There is no legislation which requires a trained first aider on work premises. The provision or otherwise of a trained first aider should be the subject of an informed decision following the completion of a First Aid Risk Assessment [*]. There is no requirement for this risk assessment to be formal or written down, however practices are strongly recommended to formalise this using the First Aid Risk Assessment [*] provided separately. Where there is no identified need to appoint a trained first-aider practices / employer should have available a copy of the risk assessment, the rationale for the decision, and be able to justify this as required later.

First aid facilities should be available at all times, and provision should include deputising in the event of absence, shift working, or if the layout of the building requires, have more than one person to adequately provide a first aid service.

Data Protection
First aid personnel must ensure that personal data, including information about individuals' health, collected during the provision of first aid is handled in accordance with the practice's Data Protection Policy.

Identification of Key Risk Factors

Consider the following factors in the provision of first aid facilities, box contents and quantities, and equipment:

The hazards identified within your workplace risk assessments, First Aid Risk Assessment, and the risk assessments conducted to ensure "safe systems of work" as required by Health and Safety legislation.
Hazardous substances and COSHH substances, use, and method of storage and transportation
Dangerous tools or equipment (including danger of body fluid splashing)
Optimum location of first aid boxes and first aid equipment
Frequency of accidents or entries in the Accident Book
The nature of all past incidents or accidents, regardless of when they happened
The number of staff / patients on site affects the quantity of items held
Staff / visitors with special needs (e.g. diabetic, partially mobile etc)
Layout of premises (e.g. if located across three floors, separate and duplicate provision may be needed on each floor)
Distance to A&E, or ambulance response times
Employees who work alone or travel (e.g. in company cars / on home visits) may require a personal first aid pack, as they are considered to be in a workplace environment.
Trainees who may be inexperienced and at greater risk
All employees being aware of where urgent equipment is kept as well as having an emergency alarm call set up by way of panic buttons in all rooms.

Training

Trained first-aiders are those who have undertaken a course of study approved by the Health & Safety Executive. There are two approved qualifications: FAAW - First Aider at Work: EFAW - Emergency First Aider at Work.

These courses must be delivered by an approved organisation.

EFAW training enables a first-aider to give emergency first aid to someone who becomes ill or suffers injury at work. FAAW training includes EFAW and also allows the first-aider to apply first aid to a range of conditions.

HSE recommends annual refresher training during the course of the three-year currency period of the qualification certificate, although this is not mandatory.

Appointed Persons do not need first aid training, as they are not first-aiders, and should not attempt to give first aid unless they are also suitably trained.

Practice Policy Summary

The following key decisions have been made in respect of first aid provision.

The practice considers that the premises are a MEDIUM risk environment based on the latest First Aid Risk Assessment

A First Aid Risk Assessment will be carried out annually, and at each significant change in the working practices, building, or personnel. This will be carried out by the appointed person and the staff representative.

The appointed person responsible for the management of the provision of first aid facilities is the practice manager

The practice has determined, following the risk assessment that no trained first-aider is required

The practice has determined that the existing clinical staff will provide first-aid where necessary to staff and patients / visitors. Under normal circumstances, there will always be at least one clinician on site.

The decision regarding the provision or otherwise of a trained first-aider has been agreed with the staff representative who is the practice manager

Staff undertaking home visits or working off site do not require a personal first aid pack

All staff will be trained in the use of resuscitation equipment annually.

The supervising GP will be responsible for medical students on the premises including risk training and avoidance of first aid hazards.

The supervising nurse will be responsible for nursing students whilst on the premises including risk training and avoidance of first aid hazards.
