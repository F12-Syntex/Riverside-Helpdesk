The Riverside Practice
Business Development Plan 2021 - 2026
1. Purpose of this Business plan
The purpose of this plan is to set out clear objectives about what we are trying to do here and to describe something about the practice and the changes we think are important to introduce over the next two years.
We hope that our staff (especially any new staff) will be able to see what we are doing, that our local Commissioning Group will feel they understand our direction and that as a business we continue to prosper in a changing world.
In producing this plan, we have learned a lot about ourselves, and we have shared a considerable amount of information among the partners and staff. We genuinely believe that we are stronger as an organisation because of this work. We consider this plan to be a "living document" and will be updated and changed as we progress through each year.
2. A brief overview of the practice and of activity
The Riverside Practice is a well-established GP Surgery that has operated in this locality since 2003. There are 5,000 patients and currently 1 male partner, a male and female sessional GP and a junior doctor who sometimes is female.
Our mission, the reason we are here, we see as:
"To provide an appropriate and rewarding experience for our patients whenever they need our support"
Our core values that are shared among the partners and staff are: -
Openness
Fairness
Respect
Accountability
The practice has always prided itself on providing high quality medicine in a traditional family practice setting and that has been the unwritten rule for many years. We believe we are respected by our peers, and we have built a positive relationship with our consultant colleagues at Homerton Hospital. Dr Goel is a GP trainer supervising Foundation Year 2 doctors and learning and education is an important part of what we do. Our practice nurse will be doing ANP training, and the practice manager has completed her HCA training.
Staffing
We have a dedicated team of staff committed to providing a first-class service to our patient population. Our staff turnover is low and is always willing to explore different ways of working. An example of this is the way we have tried to maintain good patient access by use of both face to face and telephone appointments with the doctor, especially for follow up queries. We believe that our patients see that their needs are now met with the flexibility we have built into our system, and our staff are to be congratulated for the way they have responded to the problems we experienced here.
All staff are regularly reviewed and have an annual appraisal where the goals of the individual, teams and practice are discussed, and agreement reached on the way forward. Regular reviews act as a way of reinforcing effective performance, highlight areas for improvement and recognise developing strengths.
Training is the key to improved staff performance. Regular training sessions are attended by all staff to ensure all clinical and administrative areas function effectively.
Premises
The practice relocated in 2008 to a new purpose-built health centre in Theydon Road. Previously we were in substandard premises on Upper Clapton Road which did not have any disabled access and had limited space. Our new premises are excellent and have allowed the practice to expand and become a teaching practice and provide more services for patients in modern premises.
Information technology
We would describe ourselves as satisfactory but with room for improvement. We have seen the great strides taken by other practices when they have invested in new technology and we are committed to developing more with IT. In line with the NHS plan we want to have more patients able to access and order repeat prescriptions, view their records and book appointments online. We have digitized our written Lloyd George notes and will continue doing this with new notes received.

Patient Services/Service Development
We have looked back at our performance and the way we have responded to the challenges placed before us over the last few years. We were pleased with the number of points awarded to us under QOF and still are, although we believe we worked hard on our clinical systems and administrative systems to make improvements. The responses made by patients under the friends and family test have been supportive and we have actioned areas of improvement identified by patients and discussed these with our patient participation group at our regular meetings.
Profitability and a changing marketplace
The new GMS contract offers us a total contract sum in order to provide appropriate care and services to the patients registered with us and it is our decision on how much to invest in providing this service (staff, premises, equipment, information technology and other areas) and how much to take out as profit. These decisions continue to challenge us, especially as funding gets tighter each year.
We appreciate that the marketplace is changing and we realise that continual cost increases are not sustainable, and that a new market will see change and consolidation. Locally we have an established GP Confederation and all the City & Hackney GP surgeries are members. This has allowed us to continue to provide additional services for our patients and improve the quality of care delivered as evidenced by management of long term conditions such as diabetes and the 'time to talk' service to name a few. This additional investment in local GP surgeries for patient services is not recurrent and is determined by the financial position of our local clinical commissioning group. We need to think how else the market may change and whether we are best geared as a relatively small unit to meet the future needs of our patients and as importantly, the partners and staff. We will continue to look at how best we can do this.
Management
Our management style is best defined as efficient, functional, professional, and informal. Our manager has been with us for many years and knows a lot of the patients by name. We try to instil a stress-free and friendly environment, which encourages employees to remain focused and project a positive attitude to the patients. A relaxed and friendly culture has developed among doctors, management and employees.
Communication
Dr Goel meets with the practice manager on a weekly basis with a pre-prepared agenda. We hold a monthly meeting with representatives of our staff and we have a fortnightly meeting with the sessional GP and the nursing team. We believe our communication is good but there is still room for improvement. We make use of our computer system to address this by storing minutes of practice meetings allowing cascading to all staff.
Skill Mix - Clinical and Administrative
A recurring theme to our re-examination of our current activity and our plans for the future has been to examine carefully why we undertake certain activities that we have taken as a "given" for many years; one of these is a re-examination of our skill mix-looking at who does what. Both clinically and administratively.
We have been examining who does what and we have surprised ourselves with what activities can be undertaken by people other than those who have traditionally worked on that role; this has resulted in significant delegation and cost savings; the ability to redeploy the resources we have released in other ways and it has improved the job satisfaction of staff who have been given greater responsibility. It is an area we continue to work on. One example is using the health care assistant to take on more routine clinical tasks.
Additional Roles
The practice is able to offer additional services provided by the PCN. These include at the Riverside Practice; first contact physiotherapy, a clinical pharmacist and social prescriber. At neighbouring practices we can offer extended hours consultations
Patient Forum
We have an established and active patient participation group and try to meet at least three times a year. We have tried to make this group representative, but we have no representation from newer population groups such as Polish patients.
Clinical Commissioning
One of our biggest challenges is Clinical Commissioning. We are a member of the City & Hackney Clinical Commissioning Group and are committed to working with them and to the ongoing development and provision of patient services in this area. As the new STP develops the challenge is how to ensure our patients' needs are continually met as we move to a much larger commissioning structure. By continuing the monthly commissioning meetings this will allow a GP voice to be heard.

City & Hackney Integrated Primary Care
We are a member of City & Hackney Integrated Primary Care which has allowed us to provide additional patient services at the practice and at neighbouring practices (stop smoking service). Being part of a larger organisation hopefully will help protect the practice from future potential threats. Access to resources to fund additional patient services is dependent on the local health economy and how this evolves especially as the CCG is now part of the NE London STP of 7 boroughs.
Primary Care Network
The practice is part of hackney Downs PCN which includes 5 local GP surgeries. This collaborative working has helped provide more local patient services including social prescribing, a clinical pharmacist, a home visiting service for shielding patients to check observations and take blood and now a new first contact physiotherapy service.
The practice meets monthly with its fellow practices and this allows ongoing shaping of services, sharing of good practice, support, collaborative working, quality improvement such as QI audits and helping practice resilience.
Summary
We recognise that we could have included much more information, but as a review it gives all our staff and others interested in our progress (the patients, our staff, business partners and financial advisers) a picture of what we are doing and some of the changes we intend to make over the coming months.
We have arrived at this stage and produced this document by consulting our staff and following a series of meetings with partners such as the PCN. It is important to keep this document up-to-date and this we intend to do on a regular basis to enable all to know what is happening, what areas of change are delayed and what changes have been achieved.
We welcome any comments and suggestions.
