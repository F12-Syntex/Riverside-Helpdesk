THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
ACCESS TO MEDICAL RECORDS
AND DATA PROTECTION

Introduction
This policy sets out how The Riverside Practice manages access to medical records and protects personal data in accordance with:
UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)
Data Protection Act 2018
Access to Medical Reports Act 1988
Access to Health Records Act 1990 (deceased patients only)
NHS Records Management Code of Practice
The practice recognises its legal and ethical duty to:
Protect the confidentiality of patient information
Ensure personal data is processed lawfully, fairly and transparently
Facilitate patients' rights of access to their health records
This policy applies to all staff, contractors and temporary workers.

3. Lawful Basis for Processing Health Records
The practice processes personal and special category data under the following lawful bases:
Legal obligation - compliance with NHS and statutory duties
Provision of health or social care - Article 9(2)(h)
Public task - delivery of primary medical services
Vital interests - where life or safety is at risk
Explicit consent - where required (e.g. thirdparty disclosures)

4. Subject Access Requests (SARs)
Patients have the right to access their personal data under UK GDPR.
4.1 Making a Request
Requests may be made:
In writing
By email
Via approved electronic systems
Requests must clearly identify the patient and the information requested.
4.2 Timescales
Requests will be responded to within one calendar month
This may be extended by up to two further months for complex requests
The patient will be informed in writing if an extension applies
4.3 Fees
No fee is normally charged
A reasonable fee may apply only if the request is manifestly unfounded or excessive

5. Children and Young People
5.1 Capacity and Consent
Young people aged 16-17 are treated as adults for confidentiality
Children under 16 may consent if deemed Gillick competent
Children aged 13+ may consent to online services under UK GDPR
Where a child is competent, their consent must be obtained before disclosure.
5.2 Parental Responsibility
Individuals with parental responsibility may request access unless:
The child is competent and objects
Disclosure is not in the child's best interests
Not all parents automatically have parental responsibility. Evidence may be required.

6. Patient Representatives and Third Parties
Patients may authorise third parties (e.g. solicitors, relatives) in writing.
The practice may refuse access if:
The patient did not fully understand the authorisation
Disclosure would cause serious harm
Thirdparty confidentiality would be breached

7. Deceased Patients' Records
Requests are handled under the Access to Health Records Act 1990.
Access may be granted to:
Personal representatives
Individuals with a claim arising from the death
Only information relevant to the claim will be disclosed.

8. Grounds for Withholding Information
Access may be refused or restricted if:
Disclosure would likely cause serious harm
Thirdparty information cannot be anonymised
The patient expressly objected to disclosure
Any decision to withhold information must:
Be authorised by the GP
Be documented with clear justification

9. Online Access to Medical Records (England)
Patients may request online access to:
Appointments
Repeat prescriptions
Coded medical records
Identity verification is required before access is enabled.
The practice may restrict access if:
There is risk of harm or coercion
Thirdparty information is present

10. Proxy Access
Proxy access may be granted:
With patient consent
Where the patient lacks capacity and legal authority is evidenced
Proxy access is reviewed regularly and may be withdrawn at any time.

11. Coercion and Safeguarding
Online access may be refused if coercion is suspected.
Patients will be asked to confirm they are not being pressured to share access.
Safeguarding concerns will be escalated in line with safeguarding policies.

12. Disclosure Methods and Safe Haven Procedures
Confidential information must only be shared via approved secure systems
NHSmail, AccuRx and approved NHS platforms must be used
Personal email accounts, WhatsApp and unencrypted systems are prohibited
Records must never be sent by fax unless no alternative exists.

13. Police and Legal Requests
Information may be disclosed:
With patient consent
With a court order
Where disclosure is necessary to prevent serious crime or harm
Only relevant information will be shared.

14. Insurance and Medical Reports
SARs requested by insurers will be provided to the patient, not the insurer
Medical reports are processed under the Access to Medical Reports Act 1988
Fees may apply and must be agreed in advance

15. Amendments and Rectification
Patients may request:
Correction of inaccurate data
Restriction of processing
Addition of a statement of dispute
Requests will be considered on a casebycase basis.

16. Data Breaches and Incidents
All data breaches must be:
Reported internally immediately
Logged as a significant event
Reported to the ICO within 72 hours where required
Communicated to patients where high risk exists

APPENDIX C - IDENTITY VERIFICATION (RETAINED FROM PREVIOUS POLICY)
The practice will verify identity using one or more of the following:
Passport or UK driving licence
Proof of address (utility bill, council tax statement)
Confirmation by known clinical or administrative staff
The Practice Manager (or delegated Access Management Lead) is responsible for overseeing identity verification and access approval.
