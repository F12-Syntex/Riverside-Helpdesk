FIRST AID RISK ASSESSMENT

Introduction

See also: First Aid Policy [*]

Consider these guidance notes when compiling the Risk Assessment Checklist below:

Staffing

For small numbers of employees, the minimum provision is an Appointed Person (not a trained first-aider) and a first aid box. For larger numbers, consider one or more trained first-aiders, additional equipment and multiple boxes strategically located, and a first aid room.

Inexperienced staff

These represent an increased risk, especially if they are medical, or nursing students, or other clinicians. Needlestick injuries are common in this group. Consider special post-exposure treatment and procedural training for their supervisory staff, and special pre-placement briefings (also see Medical Student Health and Safety Handbook [*]).

Low Level Hazards

If the practice has low level hazards such as occur in normal office premises the minimum requirements (as above) are likely to apply.

Shared Premises

Co-ordinate first aid provisions across buildings or parts of buildings to ensure adequate provision of first aiders and first aid boxes in each part of the building, to be suitable for quick access in the event of need. This may include separate provision of facilities on each floor of a multi-floor building. Liaise with other organisations to assess first aid need (e.g. clinics or community staff sharing health premises). A written agreement between occupants is recommended, or a shared protocol document.

Emergency services

Rural or remote areas may have a slow ambulance response time. Take this into account when assessing the need for trained first aiders.

Remote / Travelling Workers / Lone Workers

Where members of staff travel off site with work, consider the provision of a personal first aid kit with training. Where a car is provided this is considered to be a workplace and a first aid kit should be carried. Where staff work on other premises make arrangements with the other occupants for a joint protocol (as above). Consider providing mobile phones to lone workers.

Patients

There is no statutory requirement to include visitors in H&S provision. Practices are recommended to do so.

Extended Opening / Out of Hours

Assess the need to ensure cover over full opening hours e.g. there may be a need to cover all working "shifts" with additional personnel, or to make additional provision for first aid kits if parts of a building are closed off at certain times of day.

Previous Incidents

Check your accident book for previous events. Events that have occurred before have the potential to re-occur. Sign off the accident book at regular intervals as evidence of your check and include reported accidents / incidents within your next H&S risk assessment.

Attending External Incidents

There is no legislative or even contractual need for staff of a GP practice to attend to incidents requiring first aid away from the premises, as this is for the emergency services. In reality, many practices will attend to incidents close to the premises when asked to do so by a member of the public. Practices in town centres or similar locations may wish to take these types of incidents into account when assessing the requirement for first-aiders or equipment.

Data Protection
The practice treats special categories of personal data collected during risk assessments in accordance with its Data Protection Policy. Information about how data is used and the basis for processing the data is provided in the practice's employee privacy notice.
