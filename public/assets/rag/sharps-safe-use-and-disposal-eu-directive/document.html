THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
SAFE USE AND DISPOSAL OF SHARPS
EU DIRECTIVE 2013 & PROTOCOL

Introduction

On the 10th May 2010 a directive was adopted by the European Union that became law on May 11th 2013. The Regulations implement aspects of the European Council Directive 2010/32/EU (the Sharps Directive) that are not specifically addressed in existing GB legislation. They have applied since 11 May 2013.

This directive sets out the minimum requirements that come with health personnel using sharps. Higher levels of monitoring should always be applied where appropriate, however.

The majority of the act form parts of other FPM protocols that practices should be already using and so the changes should be minimal.

Outlines of the Directives

Employees now have a duty to notify the employer if they receive a needle stick injury as soon as possible.

All employers are responsible under existing Health & Safety legislation to ensure that there is an adequate assessment of the risk of injuries from Sharps, and that appropriate protection and control mechanisms are in place.

The employer must offer affective training that clearly highlights the risk of injury and good practice as well as encouraging those healthcare workers at risk to be up to date with their vaccinations in the case of injury.

There should be a follow up procedure to monitor staff that have had such injuries and a full documentation of the incident should be made. (See Significant/Critical Event Toolkit [*])

Under this directive, all employers must conduct a risk assessment and have in place adequate control mechanisms.

This should include
The risk from injuries involving medical sharps
Relevant legal duties on employers and employees.
Good practice in preventing Injury
The benefits of clawback of vaccination
The support available to an injured person from the employer.

Procedure

Regulation 6: There must be information made available to employees on;
The risk of injuries from medical sharps
Legal obligations of employers and employees
Best practice in preventing injury from sharps
Support available from employers for persons suffering an injury from sharps

Regulation 6(4): Training should be available to ensure that employees know how to work safely and without risks of injury from the specific sharps equipment. Under the Sharps Regulations, there must be training that covers;
Correct use of safer sharps
Safe use & disposal
Actions following a sharps injury
Employers' health & safety audit covering sharps

Regulation 7(1): Employers must make a record of the injury sustained when they have been made aware of it. An investigation must be carried out and a summary record made that contains;
What type of sharp involved
How severe the injury is
What stage of a procedure the injury occurred

These details can be recorded in the Practice's Accident Book, along with any other pertinent information that should be recorded in the Accident Book.

Regulation 8: The employee who receives a sharps injury in the workplace must notify the employer as soon as possible. The incident must be recorded and a full investigation must be undertaken. This must be also recorded in the Health and Safety accident book.

In many areas there is now an online reporting system such as Datix, to be sent to the CCG. This should not be seen as negative but more as a way for the CCG to monitor trends.

Regulation 7(1): The Employer must make a record of the sharps injuries when notified, and to investigate the circumstances and causes of the incident.

SHARPS SAFE USE AND DISPOSAL PROTOCOL

The risk of injury or infection from accidents with sharps within general practice is significant. The purpose of this protocol is to provide some basic rules relating to the handling and disposal of sharps.

Sharps must not be passed directly from hand to hand, and handling should be kept to a minimum.

Needles must not be bent or broken prior to use.

Always dispose of sharps at the point of use in a suitable container.

Needles must not be re-sheathed by hand.

Syringes/cartridges and needles should be disposed of intact.

Ensure that the sharps containers comply with BS 7320:1990 'Specification for sharps containers' and/or are type-approved in accordance with the Carriage of Dangerous Goods (Classification, Packaging and Labelling) and Use of Transportable Pressure Receptacles Regulations 1996

Do not fill sharps containers above the manufacturer's marked line.

Lock the used sharps container when ready for final disposal in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions.

Sharps containers should be disposed of when the manufacturer's marked line has been reached or at the intervals specified by local procedures.

Sharp bins would normally be placed under a contract with a service provider that empties/replaces the sharp bins on a weekly basis. As part of the contract, a paper document will usually be offered on collection describing the containers collected. This record must be kept by the practice.

Always carry used sharps containers by the handle.

Do not dispose of sharps with other clinical waste.

Do not place used sharps containers in yellow bags for disposal.

Place damaged used sharps containers into a large secure rigid container, which is properly labelled.

Health workers on home visits must have proper sharp bins within their mobile kit when doing home visits. And they should at all times see that the equipment is secure and away from other individuals.
