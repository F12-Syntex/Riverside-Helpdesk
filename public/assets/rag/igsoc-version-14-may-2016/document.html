INFORMATION GOVERNANCE STATEMENT OF COMPLIANCE (IGSOC VERSION 14, MAY 2016)

Introduction
The Information Governance Statement of Compliance (IGSoC) process is the procedure by which organisations such as General Practices enter into an agreement with the Health and Social Care Information Centre to access national services through the NHS National Network (N3), and its successor (N4). The terms and conditions of access are set out in the IG Assurance Statement, which is a required element of the IG Toolkit. It is essential that every organisation completes and meets the requirements of the IG Toolkit, and complies with the IG Assurance Statement to the required standards.
The IG Assurance Statement includes:
The Requirement that no Patient Identifiable Data or other sensitive data be stored or processed offshore where the location is deemed non-compliant with the HSCIC Offshore Policy
The right to audit by HSCIC or nominated third parties
Change Control Notification procedures and approvals processes
The requirements for reporting security events and incidents.

The IGSoC process is completed only once, but compliance is reconfirmed through the annual self assessment of the IG toolkit standards and the annual acceptance of the IG Assurance Statement.

Each year the practice should log in and update the toolkit. Currently we are on Version 14, which was updated in May 2016 and needs to be completed by March 31st 2017.

A summary of changes to the latest version can be found by clicking here.

In broad terms, you will be asked to complete a self-assessment form using the data you have in other protocols to ensure that you have achieved, or are working towards achieving, full compliance with Information Governance in the following areas:

Information Governance Management - this covers responsibility for Information Governance (an IG Lead), suitable policies, training for staff and staff contracts having clearly identified Information Governance requirements.

Confidentiality and Data Protection Assurance: - this covers confidentiality, consent processes, and a suitable practice leaflet which informs patients how their information is used, who may have access to that information and their rights to see and obtain copies of their records.

Information Security Assurance: this covers smart cards, information asset register, mobile computer processes, business continuity plan and procedures, and incident management and reporting procedures.

When you complete the assessment, there is an option for you to upload policies/documents etc. to evidence your compliance.

To Complete the IG SoC Process

Register with and complete the IG toolkit. (Most GP practices are already registered)

If you have not carried out an IG toolkit assessment before, follow the step by step user registration process on the IG toolkit website: www.igt.hscic.gov.uk.

If you need guidance in completing the assessment read through the "How to Complete your Toolkit User Guide" on the toolkit HELP Menu or via https://www.igt.hscic.gov.uk/resources/UserGuide-HowToRegister.pdf

Once you have completed the toolkit and are content that the scores are an accurate reflection of your attainment against the requirements, it should be signed off by senior management in your practice.

When you click the "publish" button you will be presented with the IG statement. Your acceptance of this statement provides evidence of your agreement to comply with the terms and conditions regarding your use of HSCIC's systems and services.
