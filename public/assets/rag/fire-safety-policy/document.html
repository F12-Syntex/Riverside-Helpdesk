THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
FIRE SAFETY POLICY

Introduction

This policy applies to all primary health care staff, including community staff, working at THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE.

Policy Statement

A Fire Risk Assessment has been carried out in conformance with the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 [England & Wales]/ the Fire (Scotland) Act 2005 [Scotland]/ the Fire Precautions (Workplace) Regulations (NI) 2001 [N. Ireland]. The Risk Assessment is maintained and updated annually by the 'Responsible Person' Dr R Goel- GP partner

When members of staff are required to evacuate the premises, they will ensure that they:

DO exit quickly and calmly by the nearest exit route.
DO go directly to open air.
DO close the door behind them.
DO NOT stop to collect personal belongings.

Training Instruction & Information

All new employees shall be given local fire safety induction training, involving how to raise the alarm upon discovering a fire, and what action to take on hearing the fire alarm. The training will also include physically walking the escape routes from the area in which the employee works, as well as identifying the location of fire extinguishers, fire exits and call points to show where designated assembly points are located. Employees will also be made aware of any local hazards they need to be made aware of, and be familiarised with the use of special evacuation equipment such as:

Wheelchairs.
Stair lifts.
Stairway evacuation chairs.

Procedure

All staff will be required to record all entries to and exits from the premises.
An "IN/OUT" record is to be completed and will be sited in reception.

All members of staff who receive visitors will ensure that the visitor's name is included on the record and that the "IN/OUT" boxes are ticked as appropriate. Staff responsible for meetings held on the premises will complete an attendance sheet before the meeting commences. This will contain the names of all "guest" attendees. In the event of an evacuation of the building, the responsible member of staff will present this sheet to one of the Fire Marshals on arrival at the assembly point.

ANY PERSON SUSPECTING OR DISCOVERING A FIRE SHOULD:

Raise the alarm by breaking the glass of the nearest fire alarm call point.
Dial 999 - ask for the Fire Brigade.
If circumstances dictate, or if ordered to do so, leave the building by the nearest available exit route.

ANY PERSON HEARING A CONTINUOUSLY SOUNDING FIRE ALARM MUST:

Leave the building by the nearest available exit route.
Go directly to the assembly point.
Follow instructions given by the nominated staff (Fire Marshals).
Never re-enter the building until instructed to do so by a Senior Staff Member or Health & Safety Officer.

Breaches of these procedures will be considered serious and may be dealt with under disciplinary measures.

Evacuation Procedures for Disabled Person(s)

Wheelchair Users

On hearing the fire alarm, the area's Fire Marshal will ensure that wheelchair-using patients evacuate the building by the nearest available exit and proceed to the assembly point. Where a clinician is involved with the patient at the time of the alarm, they will assist in the evacuation in normal circumstances.

Deaf/Hearing Impaired Person(s)/Patient(s)

Visual signs are situated throughout the building. Deaf or hearing impaired persons are encouraged to advise colleagues if they are likely to be working in an isolated/quiet area, so that they may be notified in the event of a fire alarm being raised.

Blind/Visually Impaired Person(s)/Patient(s)

Blind and visually impaired persons should, with their nominated assistant, agree a procedure for evacuation in the event of a fire alarm being raised.

Evacuation Drills

In accordance with fire safety legislation, The Practice Manager will carry out regular fire evacuation drills to monitor the effectiveness of local evacuation procedures, and work to improve them if it is found to be necessary.

List of Officials

Nominated Fire Marshals: Practice Manager
Deputy Fire Marshalls: Senior Receptionist on duty

Duties of the Nominated Fire Marshall or Deputy

In the event of a fire, a nominated Fire Marshall or deputy will confirm that the fire brigade has been called by calling 999. The nominated Fire Marshall or deputy will proceed to the assembly point. They will determine whether all members of staff and guests have been accounted for. Upon the arrival of the fire brigade, the nominated Fire Marshall or deputy will make him/herself known to the fire officer in charge, and pass on any relevant information, such as if any persons are not accounted for.

Duties of Fire Marshal

Standard duties:

Ensure that safe systems of work are established with regards to fire safety.
Check that the fire-fighting equipment is well maintained and in position.
Monitor that the fire alarm is tested on a regular basis.
Report any defects that may compromise fire safety.
Report any obstructions to fire doors or fire escape routes.
Ensure that new members of staff are made aware of the fire procedures, means and direction of evacuation, and location and operation of fire exit doors.
Report any defects to the practice manager/Dr Goel.

During an emergency:

As above the Fire Marshall or their deputy will confirm that the fire brigade have been called and proceed to the assembly point outside the main entrance.
The Senior receptionist on duty will check and evacuate the Riverside Practice ground floor rooms and common areas and in their absence The GP on duty will carry out this task.
This will involve knocking on each room door declaring there is a fire and to ask to evacuate the room and building.
The next senior receptionist will check and evacuate the Riverside Practice first floor rooms and common areas and in their absence the receptionist on duty will carry out this task
Ensure that the alarm has been sounded.
If possible, locate the person raising the alarm and determine the extent of the fire.
Ensure that all members of staff in your immediate area are aware of the emergency.
Ensure that those staff leave the building immediately and in an orderly fashion.
Ensure that the doors are closed as members of staff leave the building.
Ensure that no persons remain in the building.
Ensure that disabled persons or those with mobility/sensory problems are assisted in evacuating the building.
If it is safe to do so, collect "IN/OUT" tick lists on the way out of the building and, where appropriate, collect attendance sheets at the assembly point detailing attendees present at meetings.
Ensure that no persons enter the building during the emergency procedure.
Make a roll call at the assembly point using the "IN/OUT" lists and meeting attendance sheets to determine whether all members of staff and guests are accounted for.

PROCEDURE FOR SWITCHBOARD OPERATOR

Ring 999 to inform Fire Brigade.
Contact nominated Fire Officer or deputy to pass on all relevant information, confirm that the Fire Brigade has been called, and leave the building.
