Cancer safety netting and bowel cancer screening protocol

The Riverside Practice is committed to the care and screening of patients at risk of cancer. The following is our protocol for cancer safety netting of all urgent 2ww referrals and bowel cancer screening:

Bowel cancer screening:

  • CEG Searches to be run monthly by cancer admin lead. This will identify those patients:

Due for bowel cancer screening invitation (ie turning 56 or 60)

  • All patients due to be sent screening kits to be telephoned and encouraged to return kits completed
  • If patients are not contactable by telephone a standard letter is to be sent
  • Patients not wanting to engage with screening to be coded
  • Those not eligible can be coded

Patients whose screening is incomplete/ spoiled/ non-response

  • All patients who have failed to return kits will be telephoned and encouraged to complete kits
  • If patients are not contactable by telephone letters will be sent
  • Patients not wanting to engage to be coded
  • Those not eligible are to be coded
  • New kits can be obtained by calling 0800 707 6060

Use the bowel cancer screening guide for a script to use when talking to patients. It also contains answers to frequently asked questions

http://www.cityandhackneyccg.nhs.uk/gp/bowel-screening.htm

Use CEG Searches for identifying patients

Use CEG Template for recording the above activity

Cancer safety netting:

  • Cancer safety netting search and auto report to be run weekly.
  • All cancer referrals to be coded using template
  • All referrals to be made within 24 hours of patient’s attendance and coded on template.
  • Check that all referrals have received an appointment and that patients are contacted to confirm that they have received their appointment date and are going to attend.
  • Check to confirm patient attended their appointment when Hospital letter received and coded on template or DNA notification. GP cancer leads to be informed of any patients who have not attended appointment.
  • Cancer template to be completed with cancer diagnosis confirmed or coded as no cancer confirmed.

3 monthly auto reports for evidence to be run at end of reporting period and sent to confederation

Urgent Radiology results sent to the practice generic email inbox:

The practice generic email inbox is checked every 2 hours during the working day for any urgent emails including urgent radiology results.

These are printed out and given to the duty doctor for action and then scanned and added to the relevant EMIS patient record.

If appropriate this generates a 2 week urgent cancer referral and the patient is informed and given details of their hospital appointment once booked.
