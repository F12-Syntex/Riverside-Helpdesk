THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
EMERGENCY INCIDENT PROCEDURE

Introduction

This procedure is to be used to alert staff that assistance is required in emergency situations.

All staff, doctors and nurses (including locums) must be made aware of the emergency system or systems in place at the surgery. These would normally include emergency buttons, a telephone alarm, or a clinical system alarm notification. Staff should be told that the alarm should be sounded in any emergency situation, and they should react to any such alarm call immediately.

Threatening situations where the alarm could be sounded include:

Verbal or physical disruption
Verbal aggression
Physical aggression or the threat of physical violence
Physical violence
A deteriorating patient situation

All staff should use their instinct if they feel a situation may develop.

See also: Panic Alarms Procedure [*]
Precautionary procedures

If a patient is known or suspected to be violent the clinician must be made aware of this before the consultation. They should then consider whether:

The receptionist should ring the consultation room after a set time period.
The clinician should sit between the patient and the door.
The clinician should be accompanied during the consultation.
It is appropriate to see that patient.

Incident in a consulting room

If the red button EMIS panic alarm is activated, this is sent to all terminals at the time logged into EMIS as an urgent red box stating who needs help The clinic rota identifies which room someone is in at the time and if there is any change then the practitioner must inform reception of the change in room.

The reception team should:
Identify location of alarm site
Telephone the practice manager/Duty GP
Close the doors and hatch door to reception (do not lock)
One receptionist should remain by the reception counter

The first alerted person should immediately proceed to the alarm site and act as an 'investigator'.
The second alerted person should act as a 'lookout'.

The 'investigator' should:
Open the door calmly
Keep the door open
Assess the situation - e.g. weapons
Ask 'can I help?'
Notify the 'lookout' of outcome

The 'lookout' should:
Act as a go-between, communicating between the incident site and the staff

If the 'investigator' enters the consulting room and closes the door, the 'lookout' should go down to the room immediately, potentially alerting another staff member to the change of position.

Serious incident in reception area/waiting room

In the event of a serious incident in the reception area/waiting room, [emergency procedure] and the practice manager/Duty GP shall be notified immediately.

They should:
Offer assistance
Assess the situation - e.g. is there a weapon, do patients need to be escorted out of the way?
Instruct people - e.g. to clear the waiting area.

The reception staff should:

Try to minimise any 'audience' of patients
Ensure that one receptionist remains by the back desk observing the situation and being prepared to call for assistance.

Non-serious incident in reception area/waiting room

If a receptionist feels threatened, either verbally or physically, or a dispute breaks out in the waiting area they should notify the practice manager as soon as possible, who will take control of the situation and advise staff as necessary.
Incident on the telephone

If a patient is verbally aggressive or threatening on the telephone, either put the call through to the administrator or manager or say 'I am sorry, I find your manner unacceptable and if it continues I am going to put the phone down', and then hang up if necessary.
Incident outside the surgery

The practice is not responsible for any incidents occurring outside the building, unless staff members are involved. The investigator should remain by the front door, which should be locked. The investigator should assess the situation and call the police if necessary.

Any patients in the surgery that want to leave should either wait until the incident is over or leave the building via the back door. It would not be appropriate to refuse to let a patient leave in these circumstances. However, attempt to identify the patient (e.g. name) so that they may be called as witnesses if required at a later date.
Patient Deregistration

It is unacceptable for patients to behave in a violent or aggressive manner towards practice staff.
Serious incident

In the event of a serious incident, the patient and any dependants will be deregistered and removed from the practice list. The practice manager will send a letter to the patient notifying them they have been deregistered

A fax will be sent to the registration department to notify them of the immediate deregistration. The incident may need to be reported to the police, and a log number be sought.
Non-serious Incident

All staff involved in the incident will meet with the practice manager to report the incident. The partners will decide within one working day whether:

the patient will be de-registered
the patient should meet with the practice manager to discuss their behaviour and be made aware that any further incidents may lead to de-registration
c) a letter should be sent to the patient outlining our concern, indicating our intention to de-register should the behaviour recur
d) all incidents should have a written report, where all involved write down the events for record purposes. It may be that a report will need to be made to the local CCG again for monitoring purposes.
Following an Incident
Reporting

All aggressive or violent behaviour should be documented in the patient's medical record. Incidents/critical incidents should be recorded in each case and notified to the practice manager. As a minimum, the following should be recorded:
Date of incident
Person reporting the incident
Details of the incident
Names and contact details of those involved
Debriefing

All staff directly involved in the incident should meet for debriefing. The purpose of debriefing is to:
Provide support
Share concerns
Assess what happened
Assess what procedures were followed, what was done well/badly
See if anything can be learnt from this incident for the future

Counselling

Counselling is available for any staff members affected by an incident. This will be provided by Dr R Goel/The Practice Manager.
