Shared Care Policy

Introduction

1.1 – Policy Statement

To ensure that any shared are with other providers is safe and in line with local ICB and CCG protocols.

1.2 – Scope

This protocol applies to:

  • All clinical staff, including GPs, nurses, and healthcare assistants.
  • Administrative staff managing patient documents.

Patients as well as being treated by their GP surgery may also be under the care of other services including NHS and non NHS. Sometimes requests are made for the GP surgery to take part in shared care such as investigations, prescribing and follow up, while still being under the care of the other provider for their condition.

Examples can include:

  • Secondary care
  • Community services
  • Private providers

1.3 – Responsibilities

GP Surgery:

Doctors to check if any request is in line with local protocols and pathways and if unsure contact the requester to discuss and clarify.

If a shared care protocol is provided then this should be checked by the pharmacy team and if safe then signed by the lead GP and sent back to the requester with a copy saved in the EMIS patient record. Examples are as below.

The prescribing team should be consulted on any medication requests to ensure they are safe to prescribe in primary care in line with the ICB guidance and that any shared care protocol is clear on responsibilities, monitoring and follow up.

Examples include:

  • Stable ADHD medication and BP, pulse and weight checks
  • High risk drugs which are stable and 3 monthly monitoring of the FBC, renal and liver function test.
  • Warfarin and the anticoagulation book being up to date and logged in the EMIS record.
