Employee Personal and Environmental Risk Assessment Form

It is the joint responsibility of line managers and staff to complete a risk assessment for those staff at higher risk of COVID-19 infection or possibly disproportionately affected by COVID-19, as set out by Public Health England (PHE) and NHS England. As not all underlying health conditions are routinely declared, staff are encouraged to speak to their managers if they feel they need a risk assessment linked to a previously undeclared issue to enable appropriate support to be provided.

The aim of this assessment is to understand the specific risks staff members may face related to exposure to COVID-19 and enable these risks to be mitigated as far as possible.

All staff are expected to undertake their role and where they are patient facing deal with patients based on their clinical needs, in accordance with their personal and professional responsibility, unless precluded based on their individual risk assessment.

Based on national guidance we are undertaking social distancing measures in order to reduce the transmission of coronavirus (COVID-19) between individuals.

Social Distancing measures:

  • Avoid contact with someone who is displaying symptoms of coronavirus (COVID-19). These symptoms include high temperature and/or new and continuous cough, a loss or change to sense of smell or taste;
  • Avoid non-essential use of public transport, varying your travel times to avoid rush hour, when possible; Work from home, where possible.
  • Avoid large gatherings, and gatherings in smaller public spaces such as pubs, cinemas, restaurants, theatres, bars, clubs.
  • Avoid gatherings with friends and family. Keep in touch using remote technology such as phone, internet, and social media.
  • Use telephone or online services to contact your GP or other essential services.

It is important that all sections of this form are completed in order to provide a comprehensive assessment of risk and agree actions and mitigations for the protection of the staff member, colleagues and patients. This form must be filled in electronically.

Summarising the Risk Assessment

Line managers should have a sensitive and comprehensive conversation with the employee. They should identify any existing underlying health conditions or risk factors that increase the risk for the employee in undertaking the role identified.

This risk assessment should be regularly reviewed through discussions between the line manager and the employee. If the line manager is unclear about any aspects of this form they should seek support from the Director of HR and Improvement.

This risk assessment should be held on the employee’s HR files and a copy given to the employee.
