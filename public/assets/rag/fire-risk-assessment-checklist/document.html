THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
FIRE RISK ASSESSMENT CHECKLIST

Below is a checklist to assist with the preparation of a Fire Risk Assessment. Use the Risk Assessment Table, which is included in the Risk Assessment Toolkit [*], to record the findings of the Risk Assessment.

IDENTIFY HAZARDS

Sources of ignition

Are all heaters fitted with suitable guards and fixed in position away from combustible materials?

Are all items of portable electrical equipment inspected regularly and fitted with correctly rated fuses?

Is the practice free of personal electrical equipment brought in by staff?

Is the wiring of the electrical installation inspected periodically by a competent person?

Is the use of electrical extension leads and multipoint adaptors kept to a minimum?

Are flexes run in safe places where they will not be damaged?

Is the building smoke-free and are the statutory warning signs in place at each entry / exit?

Are there any potential sources of ignition from outside the building? (Adjoining buildings, storage or other activities carried on near to building)

Sources of fuel

Is the upholstery of furniture in good condition? Note: old and dilapidated furniture can contribute to the spread of fire, and torn upholstery exposes combustible filling material that may be used as kindling material by a potential arsonist. All new upholstered furniture for non-domestic use should comply with the requirements of British Standards 7176, 1995 and BS 7177, 1995.

Is the workplace free of rubbish and combustible waste materials?

Have measures been taken to ensure that smoke and flames cannot spread from one compartment/area within the building to another, e.g. are there automatically closing internal fire doors?

Is there a system for controlling the amounts of combustible materials and flammable liquids / gases that are kept in the building?

Are any combustible materials and flammable liquids and gases stored safely? Are flammable items stored in suitable vessels / cupboards designed for that purpose and are they suitably labelled?

Do procedures and practices avoid the use of combustible materials or processes that use heat?

Is any heating equipment or electrical equipment left on for longer than is needed?

Sources of oxygen

Is an air conditioning system present?

Is there a supply of medicinal / emergency oxygen on the premises?

IDENTIFY PEOPLE AT RISK

Staff

Do any staff members work alone at any time?

Do any staff members work near to sources of ignition / combustible materials?

Do any staff members work in areas that are difficult to exit from?

Patients - especially any who are:

Infirm or disabled

Elderly

Very young

CHECK FIRE DETECTION AND WARNING

Is there an automatic fire detection system and alarm system?

Is the fire alarm system in good working order?

Is the fire alarm tested weekly? (See Checklist below)

Can the fire alarm be raised without placing anyone in danger?

Are the fire alarm call points clearly visible and unobstructed?

Is the fire alarm system connected to a monitoring centre which calls the fire brigade?

Is the fire alarm system, and all its components, continuously monitored?

Are staff aware of the method of contacting the monitoring centre?

CHECK FIRE FIGHTING EQUIPMENT

Are an adequate number of suitable fire extinguishers provided?

Are the fire extinguishers and/or fire blankets located suitably and ready for use?

Are the fire extinguishers serviced annually by a competent contractor?

Are any fixed fire-fighting installations or automatic fire-detection systems in working order?

Are fire extinguishers sited in positions that are accessible in the event of a fire (e.g. close to room doors, in corridors etc.)?

CHECK SAFE ROUTES FOR EXIT

Is there a sufficient number of exits of suitable width (e.g. to allow for wheelchairs) for the people likely to be present in the building?

Do the exits lead to a place of safety?

Are all gangways and escape routes free from obstructions?

Are the escape routes free from tripping and slipping hazards?

Are steps and stairs in a good state of repair?

Are final exits always unlocked when the premises are in use, or are the devices securing final exits capable of being opened immediately and easily without the use of a key, e.g. break-glass bolts which are released by breaking a glass tube?

Are internal fire doors labelled as such, and normally kept closed?

Are the self-closers on fire doors operating correctly?

Do the doors on escape routes open in the direction of travel (i.e. towards the escape route)? Note: normally doors on escape routes should open in the direction of travel.

Are escape routes clearly signed?

Are escape routes adequately lit?

Where escape lighting is installed, is it in working order and is it maintained regularly?

Are the escape routes suitable for wheelchair use, or are wheelchair facilities available in the event of evacuation?

CHECK PLANNING AND ADMINISTRATION

Are there reminders in place to turn off all heat-producing equipment at the end of the working day?

Have plans been made and rehearsed regarding assisting disabled staff, patients and visitors to evacuate the premises?

Have suitable measures been taken to protect against the risk of arson? Note: arson is often an apparently motiveless crime, prompted merely by the availability of combustible materials, and its presence will increase the likelihood of an arson attack. All rubbish and combustible waste should be kept away from the building including bins / skips.

Have staff been trained in how to call the fire brigade, the use of the fire extinguishers and basic fire prevention?

Have you asked your insurers for advice regarding the fire protection of your premises (many insurers have free advice booklets for policyholders)?

If you employ five or more people, have you recorded the findings of the fire risk assessment?

Have you told your staff or their representatives about your findings?

If you have prepared a formal report, has it been shown to your staff or their representatives?

If you share the workplace with others, do they know about the risks that you have identified?

If you do not have direct control over the workplace, have you made your findings known to the owner or landlord?

Are fire action notices displayed prominently throughout the workplace?

Has an emergency plan been drawn up in case of a major fire?

Is a copy of the emergency plan kept somewhere other than at the workplace?

Has a procedure been established to review the fire risk assessment periodically?

Action: Record findings on the Risk Assessment Table, which is included in the Risk Assessment Toolkit [*]
