THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
INCIDENT MANAGEMENT PROCEDURE

Introduction

The purpose of this document is to define the procedure in the event of an incident at the practice.

Procedure

Should an incident occur, the following procedure should be followed?

Alert the Practice Manager or GP that an incident has occurred
Deal with the immediate needs of the incident
Complete a Significant Event Record form - all staff involved to complete a separate form without conferring and as a priority whilst the incident is fresh in the mind. Ensure that it is dated and timed for future reference
Manager will review the incident using the forms and deal with any further needs immediately arising
Submit form as an agenda item for review at the weekly partners / clinical / staff meeting
Assign any specific item requiring action to an individual for completion
When completed, pass the form to the Practice Manager for sign off
File into the risk folder located in the Practice Managers' office ready for periodic review
Consider whether this is a matter reportable to CQC
Consider whether the incident needs to be reported to the CCG and if they have an online reporting tool such as Datix make the report as soon as possible

Review

Events documented should normally be selected for review 3-6 months following resolution of the issue. A copy of historical reviews will be placed in a file maintained for that purpose.
