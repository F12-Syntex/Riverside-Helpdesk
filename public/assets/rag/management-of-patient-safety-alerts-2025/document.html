BACKGROUND
The Central Alerting System (CAS) is a web-based cascading system for issuing patient safety alerts, important public health messages and other safety critical information and guidance to the NHS and others, including independent providers of health and social care.
Alerts available on the CAS website include National Patient Safety Alerts (from MHRA, NHS England and NHS Improvement and the UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA)), NHS England and NHS Improvement Estates Alerts, Chief Medical Officer (CMO) Alerts, and Department of Health & Social Care Supply Disruption alerts.
The Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) is also now an accredited issuer of National Patient Safety Alerts (NPSA).
NPSA are official notices giving instructions to NHS bodies on how to prevent risks which might cause serious harm or death. The alerts are categorised on the immediacy of action and also state who the relevant groups to take action are.
PROTOCOL

Patient Safety Alerts will be received by the GP Lead/practice manager from the alerting bodies. After an initial review, the alert will be cascaded to all the clinicians.

The Pharmacy team will undertake a review of the alert and will decide on the actions to be taken in response to the alert.

Relevance of an alert and whether action needs to take is stated within the alert under the following subsections:

Medical Speciality
Advice for healthcare professionals
Advice for patients

The Practice Pharmacist will be responsible for ensuring the actions stated are undertaken.

Against the actions undertaken, a named person and ther role must be documented including the date it was carried out.

All relevant alerts team MUST be discussed at the practice clinical meeting:

Discussion of the alert MUST be documented within the clinical meeting minutes.
The actions to be undertaken needs to be agreed as a team.
The clinical meeting minutes must document who was in attendance.

Once the actions from the alert have been undertaken, the alert must be signed and dated as completed by either a Lead GP/ Practice Pharmacist

All details relating to the individual alert needs to be recorded on the MHRA/CAS alerts spreadsheet which must be saved on the shared drive.

NB: For alerts that require long-term ongoing action, processes will be created to ensure the practice continue to follow the actions stated within the alerts e.g. emis alerts/ pop-ups.

ADDITIONAL INFORMATION

Your Practice Pharmacist will lead on the implementation and undertaking of the actions stated in the alert, however, the practice manager and Lead GP must take responsibility for ensuring the information and required actions are made known to all relevant staff.

If your Practice Pharmacist is on leave at the time an MHRA/CAS alert is received, it is the responsibility of the practice manager and/or Lead GP to ensure that the actions required are completed and documented.
