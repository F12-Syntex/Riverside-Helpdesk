THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
LOCKING-UP PROCEDURES/KEY HOLDER DETAILS

INTRODUCTION

This protocol is to be followed when securing the premises for the evening.

Locking up Procedure
Security is everyone's responsibility but ultimately the responsibility lies with members of staff on duty at the end of the day. Before the surgery closes at a final security check should be carried out.

Windows
When a room is vacated for a period of time, the windows must be closed. The person using the room should do this.

Doors
Where key coded door locks have been fitted, these should be activated if the room is vacated for a period of time. The person using the room should do this.

Fire Doors
Fire doors must be closed or locked (where this is indicated). Care must be taken to ensure fire doors are not locked while the building remains occupied other than on final exit, where locking is required. Where there is doubt whether a fire door should be locked the Fire Service should be consulted and their advice must be followed.

Communal Offices
When working in a communal office, if you open a window you should close it at the end of your shift. If your colleagues would prefer the window to be left open, this responsibility then transfers to them - you must check this with them when you leave.

Security Check
At the end of the day, just before the surgery closes, a security check must take place. Those working the late shift should do this.

Electrical Heaters

Check ALL electrical heaters have been turned off and unplugged at the end of the day

Upstairs
The members of staff working upstairs at the end of the day should carry out part of the security check.

Check that all windows have been closed (all upstairs windows should have been closed by those using the offices)

Check that all computers and photocopier have been turned off. Turn off the lights and close the doors.
Check the toilet lights are off and close this door. Turn off the light and hot water boiler in the kitchen and close the door. Check the window in the staffroom is closed, turn off the light and close the door.

Downstairs
The member of staff working at the reception desk should carry out the security checks for the ground floor of the building. Assistance from other members of staff can be obtained if necessary.

During the course of the evening, consulting rooms can be checked and locked as soon as vacated. This will mean a gradual shut down of rooms rather than a long series of checks at the end of the day, which may delay your leaving. Along with the usual administrative tasks, the windows should be checked, the computer and other electrical items should be turned off, and the door locked using the key coded lock. Although this should have been done by the user of the room, don't assume that is has been done, doing so may leave the building in a vulnerable condition. Repeat these steps for all consulting rooms on the ground floor.
Windows in the ground floor staff and patient's toilet should be checked, as should the windows in the waiting room. The front door must be locked.
Telephones - switch over to night services at 6:30pm
Burglar alarm must be set (check that everyone is out of the building first). The door to the Admin Office must be locked using the key coded lock as you leave and all members of staff should leave via the back door, ensuring that the door has closed securely behind them. If any other members of staff are working on later than 6:30pm the burglar alarm should not be set, but these people should be informed that you are leaving the building. It is the responsibility of the last person leaving the building to set the burglar alarm.

Lone Working
It is preferable that Clinical Staff are not left alone in the building when they have a patient with them. However, if the Duty Doctor is running late and he/she has several patients still waiting to be seen, you may check with them to see if they are happy to be left alone. Depending on who is running late and who else will be in the building after the Admin Team are due to leave, arrangements may have to be made for a member of staff to work on until the patient leaves.
