The Duty Doctor service provides GP telephone advice to patients within 2 hours for all urgent requests and responds to all urgent requests from health and social care professionals within 30 minutes. Where possible, urgent calls from acute services (A&E and the London Ambulance Service should be responded to immediately).

The Duty Doctor is on call from 08:00 – 18:30 Monday – Friday (excluding bank holidays)

Health & social care professionals with urgent requests should contact the Duty Doctor via the bypass number 020 3962 4521. The IPC will be notified of any changes to this number via email to: nelondonicb.gpcontracts@nhs.net

Examples of urgent calls from patients include, but are not restricted to:

  • Symptoms of sepsis
  • Pregnant and abdominal pain or bleeding
  • Chest pain
  • Suspected stroke
  • Possible acute illness/ injury needing A+E assessment
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Allergic reaction
  • Cauda equina symptoms
  • Bleeding
  • Mental health emergencies such as suicidal thoughts

As well as other acute medical symptoms or an unwell patient

Examples of urgent calls from professionals include, but are not restricted to:

These include from:

  • The hospital
  • Social services
  • Community services
  • Coroner
  • District nurse
  • Care agency dealing with a patient
  • Other NHS services dealing with a patient

System for logging and auditing urgent calls

  1. Urgent requests made by patients
  2. Via the telephone

Urgent patient calls for the Duty Doctor should be added to EMIS via a dedicated Duty Doctor - patient appointment slot. There are dedicated pink coloured duty doctor slots in the EMIS appointment page every morning and afternoon to be used by staff who add any urgent calls to this list and inform the duty doctor.

All calls should be logged on EMIS for audit purposes, even if the call is passed straight through to the Duty Doctor.

  • Upon receiving an urgent call from a patient, the receptionist will be required to do the following:
  • The duty doctor has dedicated pink duty doctor appointment slots both am and pm each day and urgent calls are added to this list and the duty doctor contacted by telephoning or urgent screen message so that they can deal with the urgent call as soon as possible. If the duty doctor is on a visit or not at their desk, then they are called on their mobile so that they can reply as soon as they can.
  • All reception staff have been trained that urgent duty doctor calls are to be sent to the duty doctor immediately to avoid any delays
  1. Via walk in for patients who do not have access to a telephone

The practice has the following system for patients who have an urgent need who walk in due to not having access to a telephone:

  • Patient comes in and reports urgent problem
  • Placed on Duty Doctor list
  • As no phone - no option of calling back so reception contacts the Duty Doctor whilst the patient in the building
  • Patient will remain in the building until Duty Doctor advises on management:
    • Patient to wait and be seen within 2 hours
    • Patient advised to return for a F2F appointment within 2 hours
  • Reception will advise patient accordingly
  1. Via online consultation

Staff check the online requests regularly every 30 minutes throughout the working day to identify any urgent requests. These are then processed in the same way and added to the dedicated pink duty doctor slots and the duty doctor informed so that they can contact the patient within 2 hours of the patient submitting their request.

To ensure patient safety when booking online, patients are also advised if an urgent medical problem then they should call the surgery number to speak to the duty doctor.

  1. Urgent requests made by health and social care professionals

Urgent health and social care professional calls for the Duty Doctor are added to EMIS via a dedicated Duty Doctor professional appointment slot.

There are dedicated pink coloured duty doctor slots in the EMIS appointment page every morning and afternoon to be used by staff who add any urgent calls to this list and inform the duty doctor

All calls should be logged on EMIS for audit purposes, even if the call is passed straight through to the Duty Doctor.

  • Upon receiving an urgent call from a professional, the receptionist will be required to do the following:
  • The duty doctor has dedicated pink duty doctor appointment slots both am and pm each day and urgent calls are added to this list and the duty doctor contacted by telephoning or urgent screen message so that they can deal with the urgent call as soon as possible. If the duty doctor is on a visit or not at their desk, then they are called on their mobile so that they can reply as soon as they can.
  • All reception staff have been trained that urgent duty doctor calls are to be sent to the duty doctor immediately to avoid any delays
  1. Response times
  • Urgent calls from patients must be responded to within 2 hours
  • Urgent calls from health and social care professionals must be responded to immediately, or within 30 minutes

Monitoring

  • As part of any staff training the duty doctor protocol and response times are explained so that staff know when and how to contact the duty doctor as soon as they receive an urgent patient or health social care call. To avoid any delays these are put through straight away to the duty doctor who then can advise.
  • The response times are randomly checked by auditing duty doctor calls booked in the dedicated pink slots to ensure the response times and protocol are working and to identify any staff training needs.

Staff Training

As part of any new staff, temporary staff and locum induction, the duty doctor protocol, pink slots and response times are explained so that there is no delay in responding to urgent calls.

As part of any new staff, temporary staff and locum induction, any changes to the duty doctor protocol are explained so that there is no delay in responding to urgent calls.

Practice Closure/ Protected Learning Time (PLT)

The Duty Doctor service remains operational during any practice closures/training/PLT etc. There will be a receptionist and Duty Doctor available during PLT to ensure all urgent patient and professional calls are managed and responded to within the timeframes.

The practice adheres to the IPC PLT guidance available on the IPC website.
