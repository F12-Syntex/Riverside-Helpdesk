THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
EMERGENCY TELEPHONE CALL HANDLING PROTOCOL
Introduction

This protocol sets out guidelines of how to respond to a telephone call from a patient requesting urgent medical attention.
Procedure

IMMEDIATELY take the name, address & phone number of the caller as well as the patient if it is not the same. Repeat the number for accuracy.

Keep calm and speak slowly and ask the following question

Note the details carefully and accurately.

If the answer to any of the above is yes, tell the caller to dial 999immediately.

If in any doubt of the caller's ability to do so, advise that you are calling 999/112 yourself and provide the necessary details

Inform the duty doctor as soon as possible, interrupt if necessary. This is by directly ringing their room and sending an urgent screen message. If needed go to their room in person to ensure that they are informed.

If the answer is no but you are still concerned, advise the patient that you will be speaking to the doctor and will be calling back shortly, or keep them on hold until you can speak to a doctor.

Note all actions taken on the patient's clinical computer record, as well as using the surgery's messaging system if appropriate.
