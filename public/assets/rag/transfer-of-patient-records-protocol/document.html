THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
TRANSFER OF PHYSICAL
PATIENT RECORDS PROTOCOL

Introduction

The purpose of this protocol is to set out the administrative procedure for the transfer in, and the transfer out, of physical patient records. Individual instructions and procedures have been generalised to ensure that they remain relevant across clinical systems.

A number of specific requirements relate to the security of patient data, including that personal identifiable information should not be downloaded onto removable devices such as memory sticks or other storage device unless it is encrypted.

Historically the Lloyd George notes have followed the patient from surgery to surgery since the early 2000s, when clinical systems were used to record and keep patient data up to date from the inception of electronic recording. As a result, most patient notes are partly electronic and partly paper.

Recently the NHS has been supporting surgeries to get every set of notes digitalised, with all notes within the Lloyd George files to be destroyed once digitisation has taken place. Until this is completely achieved across the country there will be two processes in place:

1. The Original Process:

Transfer In

MRS IQRA KHAN, THE ACTING PRACTICE MANAGER is responsible for receipt of all transferred-in patient paper records.
On receipt of paper records from the health authority, the status of the patient on the clinical system should be altered to record that the medical record has been received (where this facility is available).
Empty the Lloyd George envelope, and arrange the record cards in chronological order.
Separate any enclosed letters. Sort into date order. Check the summary printout to ensure that all attached documents listed on the printout have been received. Request any missing documents.
Destroy any temporary discharge letters.
Pass the records to DR GOEL for summarising (see Summarising Patients' Notes Procedure [*])
After summarising, the records are passed to the Immunisation clerk for extraction and checking of the clinical immunisation record.
After the immunisation check, female records are passed to the Cytology clerk for verification of the cytology status of the patient.
The records are then filed away appropriately.

Where a storage device is received with the paper record this will contain attachments. The contents should be printed off in date order and the procedures above for the verification of all documents back to the summary printout should be followed.

Attach the storage device contents to the patient record:

Place the storage device into the drive.
Select the patient from within the clinical system and view the medical record.
Select the function used to make attachments to the record (this may be an option within the scanning menu) and navigate to the relevant drive.
Select the documents from the storage device and attach them to the patient record (see Scanning Documents Into Clinical System Protocol [*])
Keep the storage device in a secure location, pending secure destruction

Note: practices are required to transfer the full patient record and incomplete records should not be accepted.

Transfer Out

Collect all paper records including those contained in any "overflow" files.
Print off all system attachments including letters, test results, Out of Hours transmissions etc.
Ensure that all documents are tidy and that they are presented in a good chronological order.
Destroy any temporary letters.
Check for any major alert messages or other endorsements within the patient record to indicate the presence of any confidential material held elsewhere (e.g. child protection correspondence, confidential notes etc) and extract these, ensuring continued confidentiality, and enclose with the remainder of the paper record in a specially endorsed envelope.
Return to the health authority, ensuring the status of the record is update as appropriate on the clinical system.

Where a practice wishes to prepare a storage device to accompany the paper records this should only be done where NHS-approved encryption software to the required standard is available for use both in the practice and in the receiving practice.

In these circumstances it will be necessary to locate the folder on the network server which contains the clinical system patient file attachments.

Insert a storage device into the drive
Select the patient on the clinical system. Note the patient number.
On the practice network navigate using Windows Explorer (or a suitable shortcut menu) to the folder on the server where the patient's file attachments are saved.
Select all of the attachments and "send to" storage device.

The practice may also wish to save a full record summary to the storage device.

Within the clinical record select the facility for a full summary of the record.
Where the option exists, select the inclusion of "all letters on the patient record" for transfer to the storage device.
Select the option to save to storage device - this may involve manually typing the pathway and name the file with the patient identifier, completing the save procedure.

2. Following Digitalisation

Once the paper notes are digitalised, surgeries will only have the Lloyd George envelope without any papers contained within. When a patient leaves the practice, the new surgery will electronically request the electronic notes from the previous surgery and the records will be electronically received.

The Lloyd George envelope will then be sent to the new practice using the normal procedure.

When a patient registers at the surgery the registration is handed in the same way on the clinical system, automatically triggering a request to the new practice to send the records electronically.

This is a far simpler method and, in theory, notes can be received within hours of registering rather than the weeks it currently takes.
