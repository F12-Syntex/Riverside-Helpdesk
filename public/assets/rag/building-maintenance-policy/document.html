RIVERSIDE PRACTICE BUILDING MAINTENANCE POLICY

Introduction

The purpose of this policy is to provide an overview of the way in which the surgery premises will be managed, maintained and repaired.

Objectives

The objectives of the Maintenance Policy are to:

clarify maintenance responsibilities for the land and building
specify the minimum requirements for the maintenance of specified parts
ensure the building is properly maintained in a timely and cost effective way
ensure that risks are managed
ensure that land and building continue to meet the operational needs of the practice
provide evidence to the CQC and other statutory bodies that the practice takes its responsibilities seriously for the prudent and compliant management of premises issues

Responsibilities

The practice person responsible for the organisation and control of the maintenance of the building is Dr R Goel. This will include the scheduling of the routine maintenance visits, liaison with other building users, and the organisation of occasional repair works.

Policy

The responsible person Dr R Goel is to be contacted to coordinate all maintenance requirements. In the event of absence, the Practice Manager is to deputise.

The responsible person will liaise with other building occupiers and building owners as appropriate, and will have the final and binding decision on all building related matters.

The following will be priority considerations in considering repair or maintenance, or improvement requests:

health and safety issues
safety of staff and public / risk management
routine or planned renewal of assets
statutory requirements
best practice requirements (e.g. enhancements in relation to disabled access etc.)
cost / benefit / disruptive aspects
public perception and appearance

These aspects will be considered in relation to whether they are:

Important, and need to be maintained to a high standard (e.g. minor surgery areas)
Fairly important to maintain to a good standard (e.g. waiting rooms and public areas for aesthetic reasons)
Fairly important to maintain to a good standard for risk reasons (e.g. steps, staircases or lifts)
Of low importance (e.g. private corridors which are otherwise safe)
Unimportant (e.g. utility areas which are otherwise safe)

Audit

A buildings and grounds inspection will be carried out as part of the routine risk assessment inspection every six months, and will form part of the standard risk assessment / hazard identification procedures.

The responsible person will assess and implement any corrective action recommended.
