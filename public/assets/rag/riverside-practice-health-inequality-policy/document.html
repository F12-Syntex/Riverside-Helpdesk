The Riverside Practice Health Inequality Policy

Practice Name: The Riverside Practice

Date Adopted: 19.9.2025

Review Date: 19.9.2026

Lead for Health Inequalities: Dr R Goel-Lead GP

1. Purpose and Statement of Commitment

We recognise that health inequalities are avoidable, unfair, and systemic differences in health outcomes across population groups. As a GP practice, we are committed to:

  • Reducing barriers to access
  • Providing equitable, person-centred care
  • Improving health outcomes for underserved groups in our community

This policy sets out our approach to embedding health equity into our practice operations, services, and quality improvement.

2. Understanding Our Population

We use local data to understand the characteristics of our patient population, including:

  • Levels of deprivation (e.g. IMD quintiles)
  • Ethnic and cultural diversity
  • Language and literacy needs
  • Inclusion health groups (e.g. people experiencing homelessness, refugees, Gypsy, Roma & Traveller populations)
  • Long-term conditions and multimorbidity prevalence

3. Access and Inclusion

We aim to remove barriers to care through:

  • Accessible appointments: Extended hours, same-day slots, telephone/online options
  • Language support: Access to interpreters and translated materials
  • Registration without ID/address in line with NHS guidance
  • Digital inclusion: Alternative booking for patients who struggle with online systems

4. Cultural Competency and Staff Awareness

We commit to:

  • Providing staff training on health inequalities, unconscious bias, and cultural competence
  • Promoting inclusive communication, including for neurodiverse and learning-disabled patients
  • Respecting patients’ cultural and religious needs during care delivery

5. Service Design and Delivery

We tailor services to address known inequities:

  • Targeted outreach for screening and immunisation (e.g. cervical, flu, COVID-19)
  • Extended reviews for patients with multiple LTCs or social vulnerabilities
  • Working with social prescribers and link workers to address wider determinants
  • Partnership with voluntary/community sector organisations

6. Monitoring and Evaluation

We monitor and review equity using:

  • Clinical and access data disaggregated by age, sex, ethnicity, and deprivation
  • Patient feedback from diverse groups
  • Outcome tracking for targeted interventions
  • Annual audit of equity in screening, prescribing, and referrals

Findings are discussed at clinical and management meetings, with QI cycles applied where needed.

7. Patient and Community Engagement

We involve patients and the community by:

  • Actively recruiting underrepresented groups to our Patient Participation Group (PPG)
  • Co-producing outreach initiatives with community partners
  • Conducting accessible surveys and listening exercises

8. Partnership Working

We collaborate with:

  • Local PCNs and ICB on other equity initiatives
  • Local authority public health and social care teams
  • Third sector organisations working with marginalised groups

9. Review and Accountability

This policy is reviewed annually and updated in line with best practice guidance from NHS England, RCGP, and CQC.

The practice’s Health Inequalities Lead is responsible for overseeing the delivery and monitoring of this policy.
