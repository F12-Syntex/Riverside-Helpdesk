1. Introduction

This protocol provides a shared framework for ensuring patient safety across all Hackney Downs PCN practices. It promotes proactive risk management, standardised procedures, and a culture of transparency and learning.

2. Purpose

To ensure safe, consistent care across the PCN by:

  • Standardising reporting and learning from incidents
  • Encouraging open, blame-free communication
  • Meeting legal and regulatory standards (e.g., Duty of Candour, CQC)
  • Supporting continuous quality improvement and staff training

3. Scope

Applies to all PCN staff, including GPs, nurses, ARRS roles, admin teams, and leadership. Everyone has a role in upholding patient safety.

4. Key Definitions

  • Patient Safety Incident: An unintended event that could or did cause harm
  • Near Miss: Incident avoided before harm occurred
  • Significant event analysis (SEA): A structured review of significant events
  • Learning event analysis: A review of an incident that is does not cross threshold of significant harm/risk of harm but is still useful for learning
  • Duty of Candour: Obligation to be open when something goes wrong
  • Risk Assessment: Identifying and addressing potential hazards

5. Roles & Responsibilities

  • PCN Clinical Director: Oversees safety culture across practices
  • PCN Development Manager: Ensures review of PCN patient safety protocol, oversees engagement of ARRS staff and third parties in patient safety procedures
  • PCN Coordinator: Supports practice communication and reviews
  • Practice Managers: Lead local implementation and incident documentation in practices including with practice-employed ARRS staff
  • Practice patient Safety Leads: Coordinate investigations if required, keep up to date on patient safety protocols, promote reporting, share learning, champion patient safety in the practice
  • All Staff (clinical & admin): Follow safety procedures, report incidents, engage in SEA meetings

6. Safety Culture

  • PCN leadership sets expectations for safety
  • Practice managers are engaged with development of patient safety protocol and champion patient safety
  • Each practice has a named patient safety lead – this is usually the practice manager or another senior member of staff
  • Staff are encouraged to report incidents without fear
  • Learning from events is prioritised over blame
  • Risk management is embedded in day-to-day operations

7. Incident Reporting

  • There are 3 levels of incident reporting:
    • Within the organisation for minor incidents
    • Nationally via NHS safety reporting for incidents of harm
    • Locally to the NEL ICB where an incident involves an external provider
  • This protocol deals with practice-based incidents, practices should also ensure that staff are aware of ICB systems of reporting (nelondonicb.qualityandsafety@nhs.net)
  • The practice safety lead should be informed of all patient incidents and can advise if an incident requires a serious incident report and an SEA; or a locally completely incident report and learning event analysis
  • All serious incidents must be reported within 24 hours using the NHS safety reporting platform (https://record.learn-from-patient-safety-events.nhs.uk/)
  • All incident reports should be shared with practice safety lead and practice manager
  • All incidents should be scheduled for a SEA or LEA meeting within the practice

8. Significant Event Analysis (SEA)

  • Practices conduct SEA and LEAs for all incidents and near misses
  • Practices ensure that all staff are aware and supported to report incidents and carry out SEAs
  • Investigations are led by staff member involved with the incident where appropriate, or if not then by the Patient Safety Lead
  • Practices schedule regular SEA meetings (minimum quarterly) and encourage all staff participation
  • Key shared learning is discussed at PCN-level meetings
  • PCN schedules bi-annual meetings to share incident learning

9. Risk Assessment

  • Risks identified through reports, audits and patient feedback

10. Training

  • Mandatory patient safety training for all staff (on induction and annually)
  • SEA and incident reporting procedures included in training
  • Training needs reviewed in annual appraisals
  • Patient safety lead maintains annual update of patient safety protocol and keeps up to date with national and local guidance
  • PCN supports practices with access to training if required

11. Communication

  • Safety updates shared through practice, PCN and patient group meetings; and practice newsletters
  • Learnings disseminated across PCN
  • Patients informed of relevant incidents per Duty of Candour

12. Continuous Improvement

  • Regular audits and reviews to track performance
  • Annual review of incidents by practice patient safety lead and feedback to leadership of practice and PCN of key themes; successes and areas of improvement

13. Record Keeping

  • Use NHS online platform for reporting and storing incident data
  • Practices maintain SEA records, audit results, and training logs
  • Share key learning at PCN meetings when appropriate

14. Legal Compliance

  • Adhere to CQC, NHS England, and GDPR regulations
  • Limit access to sensitive data to authorised personnel

15. Protocol Review

  • Reviewed annually by PCN leadership and practice managers
  • Updates shared with all staff
  • Training provided on any changes
