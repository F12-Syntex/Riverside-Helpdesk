Zero Tolerance Policy – The Riverside Practice

1. Introduction

At The Riverside Practice, we are committed to providing high-quality healthcare in a safe, respectful, and inclusive environment for all patients, visitors, and staff. We have a legal and moral duty of care to protect our team from any form of abuse. We therefore operate a Zero Tolerance Policy towards aggressive, violent, abusive, or disrespectful behaviour.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all individuals interacting with The Riverside Practice, including patients, carers, and visitors, whether that interaction is in person, over the telephone, or in written/digital communications (e.g. email, SMS, social media).

3. Unacceptable Behaviour

Unacceptable behaviour includes, but is not limited to:

  • Shouting, swearing, or using aggressive, abusive, or offensive language
  • Verbal or written threats to staff or other patients
  • Intimidation, harassment, or bullying (including undermining or repeatedly challenging staff decisions)
  • Making malicious or defamatory remarks about staff or the practice
  • Repeated derogatory comments (including on social media)
  • Entering staff-only or clinical areas without permission
  • Demanding to speak only to specific staff or refusing to accept appointments with appropriate clinicians
  • Persistent unrealistic demands that cause undue stress to staff
  • Contacting staff through personal or unofficial channels (e.g. social media, personal email)
  • Attending under the influence of alcohol or illegal substances
  • Tampering with or altering prescriptions
  • Theft, damage to property, or any criminal act on practice premises

This list is not exhaustive. Any behaviour that makes staff feel unsafe, intimidated, or disrespected will be considered under this policy.

4. Staff Rights

All staff at The Riverside Practice have the right:

  • To be treated with dignity and respect at all times
  • To carry out their duties without fear of abuse or harassment
  • To work in a safe environment

We expect our staff to act professionally, respectfully and sensitively at all times – and expect patients to show the same courtesy in return.

5. Policy and Procedure

Step 1: Verbal Warning (Discretionary - Minor Incidents)

For a first-time or isolated minor breach of this policy, the practice may choose to issue a verbal warning, clearly advising the patient that their behaviour was inappropriate and must not be repeated.

However, where concerns are not serious or where a single incident may be open to interpretation, the practice may choose to monitor the situation rather than issue a verbal warning immediately. This is to ensure patients are not unfairly judged or feel they are being treated with prejudice.

Any decision to issue a verbal warning will be made in context and at the discretion of the Practice Manager or senior clinician. Where issued, verbal warnings may be recorded internally for future reference.

Step 2: Written Warning (Repeat or Serious Breach)

A written warning will be issued:

  • If the inappropriate behaviour is repeated, or
  • If there is more than one incident, even if each is minor, or
  • If the initial breach is deemed serious (e.g. abusive language, intimidation, or threats)

Amended clause (added):
Where multiple incidents have been noted internally and considered cumulatively as part of a pattern of behaviour, the practice reserves the right to issue a formal written warning without prior written notice, particularly where earlier concerns were managed informally or with verbal reminders.

The warning letter will:

  • Describe the incident(s)
  • Reference the Zero Tolerance Policy
  • State that any further incident may result in removal from the practice list
  • Offer the patient an opportunity to respond or meet with the Practice Manager

Step 3: Immediate Removal

Immediate removal from the practice list will occur where there is:

  • Any form of physical assault or serious threat
  • Police involvement
  • Severe abuse that compromises staff safety or significantly breaks the doctor-patient relationship

The patient will be notified in writing and referred to NHS England, and if appropriate, placed under the Special Allocation Scheme.

Step 4: Household Removal (If Applicable)

In rare situations, it may be necessary to remove other members of the household if continuing their care would pose a risk to staff safety or wellbeing—particularly where the patient removed poses an ongoing threat or risk of confrontation.

6. Appeals Process

Patients who have been removed following a written warning may request an appeal by contacting the Practice Manager within 14 days. Appeals will be reviewed internally, but final decisions rest with the Lead GP, in line with NHS policy.

7. Complaints

We encourage patients to raise concerns or dissatisfaction through our formal complaints procedure, not through disruptive or abusive behaviour. Complaints will be handled professionally and without prejudice.

8. Summary

The Riverside Practice is proud to serve our patients with compassion, professionalism, and respect. We ask that patients treat our team with the same values. This policy is in place to protect our staff and maintain a safe environment for everyone.

9. Warning letter Template

Dear [Patient Name],

I am writing to formally address concerns regarding your recent behaviour towards staff at The Riverside Practice.

It has been brought to our attention that on [insert date(s)], and on [insert any relevant previous occasions if applicable], you [brief description of the incident(s), e.g., spoke to a member of the reception team in a disrespectful and undermining manner / used inappropriate or aggressive language during a telephone conversation / displayed intimidating behaviour during a consultation, etc.].

This type of behaviour is not acceptable and is in direct breach of our Zero Tolerance Policy, which applies to all interactions with practice staff. The policy is in place to ensure that our staff can work in a safe and respectful environment, free from abuse, aggression, and intimidation.

While we acknowledge that patients may sometimes feel upset or frustrated, this does not justify behaviour that causes distress to staff or disrupts the delivery of care.

As a result, we are issuing this letter as a formal warning. Please be aware:

  • Any further incidents of inappropriate or disrespectful behaviour may result in your removal from our patient list.
  • This decision is supported by our Zero Tolerance Policy and NHS England guidance on maintaining a safe and respectful healthcare environment.
  • A copy of this warning will be recorded on your patient file for internal reference.

If you believe there are misunderstandings or if you wish to discuss this matter further, you are welcome to contact the Practice Manager. We are open to having a constructive and respectful conversation.

We remain committed to supporting your healthcare needs and providing a high standard of service. Your cooperation moving forward is essential and appreciated.

Yours sincerely,

The Riverside Practice
