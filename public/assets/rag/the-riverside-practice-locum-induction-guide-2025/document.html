1. Arrival and Orientation

On arrival, we will:

  1. Introduce you to the key staff.
  2. Show you around the practice:
    • Emergency equipment (see Section 3)
    • Consulting rooms
    • Reception and admin area
    • Kitchen (tea/coffee facilities)
    • Fire Exits and Meeting Point
  3. Discuss how you will hand over tasks (paper, EMIS Tasks, face-to-face).
  4. Highlight referral forms (e.g. Fast Track 2WW for cancer, chest pain, TIA).
  5. Inform you of tasks like visits, prescriptions, and patient reviews early in your session.
  6. Show you how to top up forms, swabs, and blood bottles.
  7. Provide login credentials for all IT systems (EMIS, TQUEST, AccuRx, Microsoft Word, and Surgery Connect).
  8. Make you aware of sample collection times and the location of the doctor’s bag and emergency equipment and fire exit roots and meeting point.

2. Working Hours and Duties

  • Consultations: Use the EMIS system.
  • Phone calls and tasks: Managed via EMIS and AccuRx.
  • Visits/Prescriptions: Confirm early in the session with the Practice Manager or Clinical Supervisor.
  • Sample Collection Times: 1:00 pm daily.
  • Clinic Timings:
    • Morning Session: 9:00 am – 12:00 pm
    • Afternoon Session: 2:00 pm – 5:00 pm

3. Emergency Procedures

Emergency Equipment Locations

  • Emergency Drugs, Oxygen, and Nebuliser: Room 4 (bottom cupboard by the window).
  • Defibrillator, First Aid Box, and Spillage Kit: Reception (opposite the entry door).
  • Doctor’s Bag: Store room (green bag on the right).
  • Spillage Kit: Reception area.

Panic Alarm

  • Use the Red Button on EMIS for emergency assistance.

Drugs Available

  • GTN, Salbutamol, Adrenaline 1:1000, Aspirin 300mg, Benzylpenicillin, Oxygen.

5. Key Responsibilities

Seeing Patients

  • Call patients using ‘S’ on EMIS.
  • Document clearly: use accurate codes, examination notes, and management plans.

Prescribing

  • Avoid unlicensed medications marked with a ‘U’ in EMIS.
  • For DMARDs and hospital-only drugs, ensure appropriate monitoring (check FBC, LFT, and U&Es).
  • Do not prescribe HIV drugs, Clozapine, Roaccutane, warfarin, or other hospital-only medications.

Investigations

  • Use TQUEST for bloods, radiology, and mycology requests.
  • Adult Bloods: Book via reception or external service.
  • Paediatric Bloods: Refer to Homerton Starlight Unit (020 8510 7425).

Results and Workflow

  • Check Workflow Tasks regularly throughout the day.
  • Use admin support for informing patients of normal results.

Referrals

  • Routine Referrals: CEG shared folder in Documents or Standard Letter Template
  • Discuss with Dr Goel before making a routine referral or send him a task
  • Also use advice and guidance for consultant advice using ERS and add the code
  • “consultant advice service used”
  • 2WW Referrals: Use PAN-London Templates.
  • Emergency Referrals: Print a consultation summary and send patients to A&E. And add code “referral to accident and emergency service”
  • Community Services: Refer to local care pathways (Teamnet or CCG website).

6. Practice Policies

  • Safeguarding: Policies are on Teamnet and on the blue board.
  • Local Prescribing Guidelines: Access via the CCG website.
  • EBI Policy: Ensure funding approval for procedures of limited clinical value.
  • Spillage and Infection Control: Follow practice protocols.
  • EMIS Home Page: Contains useful contact information for local services.

7. Practical Essentials

  • Store Room: Code CX2479. Stock includes:
    • Urine/stool bottles
    • Swabs and speculums
    • Batteries and printer supplies
  • Chaperone: Ask the HCA ( Iqra ) or Practice Nurse ( Mishalu ) .
  • Forms, Swabs, and Sample Bottles: Replenish as needed from the store room.

8. Communication and Conduct

  1. Handover: Clarify handover format (EMIS Tasks, face-to-face).
  2. Confidentiality: Follow NHS data protection guidelines.
  3. Delays/Absences:
    • Inform Dr Goel and Iqra by 9:00 am.
    • Call reception as early as possible.
  4. Emergencies: Follow protocols outlined in Section 3.

9. Key Actions for Your First Day

  1. Log into all systems: Windows, EMIS, TQUEST, AccuRx, Surgery Connect, and Microsoft.
  2. Familiarise yourself with consulting rooms, store room, and emergency equipment.
  3. Bookmark useful sites:
    • NICE: cks.nice.org.uk
    • Patient Info: patient.co.uk
    • Local Pathways: gps.cityandhackneyccg.nhs.uk
  4. Review handover and referral processes.
  5. Confirm sample collection times and procedures.
