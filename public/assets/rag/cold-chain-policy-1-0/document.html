THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
Cold Chain Policy

All members of staff will follow the current CCG Mandatory Cold Chain policy. This policy shall be referred to for all vaccine management issues and in-house annual cold chain audit
Staff Responsibilities

All members of staff involved in the handling and management of vaccine stock and fridges will have initial training and annual updates, as per HPA recommendations.
All qualified nursing staff to ensure they maintain their professional responsibilities and attend regular updates (recommended yearly) regarding immunisation and Cold Chain Policy.
All members of staff are to understand their roles and responsibilities in the event of a Cold Chain incident and ensure this is dealt with appropriately to minimise risk and potential vaccine wastage.
Designated trained staff are to check all two vaccine fridges daily, documenting the date, current reading, highest temperature and lowest temperature on the fridge temperature record chart. These records must be kept for eleven years. The names of the designated staff will be displayed on the fridges and within the practice policy.
Fridge Management

Vaccine fridges must be properly maintained, kept clean and tidy, not over-stocked (which prevents air flow and poor circulation) and calibrated annually.
Our main vaccine fridge will have two methods of monitoring fridge temperature (as per HPA and Cold Chain Policy guidelines) these will both be calibrated for accuracy annually.
A Cold Chain audit will be completed bi-annually by a designated practice nurse (PN).
Fridge temperature management records will be audited quarterly by a PN.
Practice nurses using the vaccine fridges must ensure that -
Vaccine stock does not block the fridge internal fan overheating the fridge.

Vaccine stock does not get pushed to the back wall of the fridge where freezing occurs.

The fridge does not become overstocked preventing good air circulation.

Vaccine stock is rotated correctly ensuring stock does not become out of date.

Vaccine stock does not become mixed up on shelves which can cause drug errors in busy clinics.

Fridge temperature is to be maintained between 2-8 degrees C, with a mid range of 5 degrees C being best practice. Action MUST be taken if temperature falls outside these parameters. Vaccine stock is under licence when stored between 2-8C, any vaccine stored outside these temperatures is no longer a licensed product.

Receipt of Vaccine Stock

On receipt of vaccine stock into the practice this must be handed to a practice nurse to be placed in the fridge straight away to maintain Cold Chain. If no practice nurse is available all staff members must understand their own responsibilities in the correct and prompt storage of vaccine stock.
Fridge temperature is to be recorded when vaccines are received into the practice and stock check completed by a practice nurse. This provides evidence that cold chain has not been disrupted during transportation of vaccine stock.
All vaccine stock received into the practice should be correctly checked by a practice nurse against the invoice details. Check quantity of each vaccine, batch number and expiry dates. Sign and date the invoice and forward this to the Practice Manager. The vaccine details will then be entered on to clinical system for electronic vaccine stock control.

Vaccine fridges will be checked daily by the reception staff
In any absence the temperature check will be completed by other trained staff including Dr R Goel, Mrs I Khan and the practice nurse Mishalu Joseph.

Unused/Expired Vaccines

If any vaccines are due to expire and remain unused, then these are disposed of in the sharps bin at the practice.

Any unused seasonal flu vaccine will either be disposed of as above at the practice or returned to the vaccine supplier for safe disposal if more than 50 in number.
