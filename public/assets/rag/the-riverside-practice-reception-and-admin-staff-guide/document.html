3. Induction & Orientation for New/Locum Staff

  • Notify staff of new arrivals, allocate mentor/work area, provide induction pack and organisation chart.
  • Tour: parking, kitchen, toilets, fire exits/extinguishers, visitors book, phones, photocopier, stationery, computer access, mail points.
  • Explain alarm codes, PPE, infection control, spillage kits, lone worker expectations, zero-tolerance statement.
  • Clarify job title, responsibilities, line manager, expectations.
  • Demonstrate EMIS, filing, telephones, leave/sickness reporting, and communication channels.

4. Learning Culture & Incident Reporting

  • Incident Reporting: Urgent EMIS task to Lead GP and Practice Manager.
  • Sharing Learning: Monthly meetings, TeamsNet updates, ad hoc emails.
  • Protected Learning Time: For training, own tasks, SARS and admin duties.
  • Raising Concerns/Bullying: Report to Practice Manager or Lead GP.

5. Registrations - Refer to the Registration Guide

  • No ID or proof of address required.
  • Homeless/boat patients: register under practice address.
  • Offer paper or online registration—patient’s choice.
  • Register within 5 working days.
  • Accept temporary and permanent registrations.
  • Out-of-area patients: advise that home visits are not available outside catchment (map on blue board).

6. Booking & Arrivals

  • Always book/manage appointments same day—never ask to call back.
  • Confirm and update contact details and preferred communication method.
  • Offer external services: Pharmacy First, MEC Clinic, NHS 111 (dental only), Enhanced Access clinics (Hackney Downs Hub, Sat all day, 7 am starts, till 8 pm on weekdays).
  • Document reasons if unable to book.
  • Check in promptly on EMIS, maintain confidentiality, and never turn away visibly unwell patients—alert duty doctor.

7. Late Arrivals Protocol

  • Over 10 mins late: Politely explain delay and send urgent EMIS screen message to clinician to check if they can be seen or rebooked.
  • Emergencies/unwell: escalate to duty doctor—do not turn away.
  • Record late arrivals in EMIS; follow up persistent lateness.

8. Duty Doctor Calls

Qualifies as duty calls: urgent symptoms, hospitals, coroners, social services, safeguarding, urgent pharmacy queries, or any case needing immediate input.

  • Keep caller on the line.
  • Transfer directly to duty doctor and add to urgent telephone list on EMIS.
  • Document call and outcome.

9. Triage & Urgent Symptoms

  • Use triage protocol to assess urgency.
  • Routine: Book same day when possible.
  • Non-urgent follow-ups: Book with same GP for continuity by sending them a task; otherwise book in with duty doctor.
  • Escalate urgent/uncertain cases immediately.
  • Document conversations and actions in EMIS.

10. Referrals

  • Routine: Action within 48 hrs, log in ERS, notify patient.
  • Two-week wait: Action within 24 hrs, book live, if possible, record details, advise follow-up if not contacted, code two-week wait template and create a diary entry of 2 week’s time.

11. Blood Results

  • Normal: If patient calls, task requesting doctor (or duty doctor if unavailable).
  • Abnormal or Urgent results: Flag urgently to duty doctor.
  • Document all actions in EMIS.

12. Prescriptions

  • Send acute/past drug requests to lead pharmacist.
  • Calculate repeat due dates and match batch issues to expected end date.
  • Monitoring Medications – send to pharmacist:
    • Warfarin (request INR & code), Apixaban, Rivaroxaban, Dabigatran, Edoxaban.
    • Methotrexate, Leflunomide, Sulfasalazine, Hydroxychloroquine, Azathioprine, Mycophenolate, Ciclosporin, Tacrolimus.
    • Lithium, Amiodarone, High-dose corticosteroids, Clozapine (sec. care), ACE inhibitors/ARBs.
  • Escalate bleeding, infection, bruising, breathlessness, or severe side effects to duty doctor.

13. Workflow Tasks & Emails

  • Routine tasks/emails: 48 hrs. Urgent: 24 hrs.
  • Code emails/documents correctly.
  • All clinical documents and results → workflow to Dr RG.
  • Assign tasks appropriately; use protected admin time.

14. Medical Records Requests (SARS)

  • Log date received and EMIS number on board.
  • Task Practice Manager and Reception Lead.
  • Complete within 30 days unless extension agreed.

15. Confidentiality & Secure Communication

  • Use EMIS numbers instead of names in shared spaces.
  • Share data only via nhs.net/gov.uk.
  • For others, gain consent and mark [SECURE] in subject of EMAIL.
  • Check consent before sending medical info.
  • Check safeguarding flags before sharing.
  • Keep Smartcards and passwords secure. Ensure you log out when absent from desk. 
  • Report errors immediately to Practice Manager.

16. Infection Control & Health & Safety

  • IPC Lead: Practice Manager & Practice Nurse.
  • Follow PPE and bare-below-elbows.
  • Specimens: Store securely, print label with date of collection, place in red box, remind patients to call for results.
  • Fridge temps: Log start/end of shifts.
  • Equipment: Oxygen (Rm 4), Defibrillator (Reception), Spill Kits (Reception), Panic Alarms (EMIS).
  • Business Community Plan: TeamsNet & Reception Board.
  • Report hazards; join fire drills.

17. Emergency Preparedness

  • Collapsed patient/suicidal caller: For Collapsed patient, Press Panic Button and for Duty Doctor Calls, alert duty doctor immediately (urgent EMIS message, call or enter their room). Check board for Duty Doctor Schedule.
  • Know AED, oxygen, spill kits, fire exits.
  • Violent incidents: Press Panic Button on EMIS lock reception, await instructions.

18. Locking-Up Procedures (Summary)

  • Check/close all windows and doors.
  • Turn off lights, computers, heaters, photocopiers, kitchen appliances (Except Fridge), staffroom equipment.
  • Switch phones to night service (6:30 pm), lock front/admin doors.
  • Exit via Front door only and not back door, ensure locked when left.
  • No lone working with patients—arrange cover or stay.

19. Safeguarding

  • Leads: Dr Rajiv Goel; backup Dr Rishi Aggarwal or Practice Manager.
  • External Contacts: blue board.
  • Receptionists complete Level 2 safeguarding.
  • Code carers, disabilities, veteran, safeguarding alerts.
  • Use safeguarding proforma/flowchart for concerns on Blue Board.

20. Equity of Access & Inclusion

  • Catchment map: blue board.
  • Out-of-area: advise no home visits.
  • Record communication needs/formats (Accessible Info Standard).
  • Offer interpreters/hearing loops/reasonable adjustments - Interview room use for breastfeeding mothers or patients requesting quieter place sensitive to noise and crowded places and use for confidential conversations.
  • Flag carers and LD/autistic patients.
  • Be aware of veterans and Safe Surgery schemes.

21. Patient Communication & Complaints

  • Complaints Lead: Practice Manager.
  • Targets: Acknowledge 3 days; respond 10 days.
  • Log all complaints.
  • Signpost to ICB Complaints if requested.
  • Share lessons learned at Practice Meetings/TeamsNet.

22. Partnerships & Community Links

  • Engage PPG for feedback.
  • Use Better Together Hackney, social prescribers, coffee mornings, walking groups, IT sessions.
  • Use PCN hubs/ARRS for Enhanced Access bookings.

23. Staff Wellbeing & Performance

  • Breaks after stressful calls; rotate duties.
  • Take regular screen breaks
  • Open-door policy with PM.
  • Therapy via OT if needed.
  • Report performance or bullying concerns to PM or Lead GP.

24. Other Key Awareness Areas

  • Clear desk: secure notes/pads.
  • Stock: report low levels.
  • Update boards/Information Folder in Reception: remove outdated info.
  • Teamwork & self-care: support colleagues, escalate when unsure, take breaks.

25. Whistleblowing & Freedom to Speak Up (Summary)

  • Guardian: Iqra Khan (Practice Manager).
  • Escalation: If involves her, go to Dr Rajiv Goel.
  • Process: Report verbally or in writing; prompt, confidential investigation.
  • External options: GMC or ICB if unresolved.
  • Protection: No victimisation for genuine concerns; malicious claims = disciplinary.
  • Follow NHS Freedom to Speak Up principles—raise issues early.

26. Extra Responsibilities & Reporting

  • Incident Reporting Folder: Log all significant events, near misses, or safety issues in the Incident Report Folder (kept in Reception). Notify the Practice Manager or Lead GP immediately via EMIS urgent task or screen message.
  • Duty of Candour Book: Record any incidents where an error or harm has occurred. Inform the Practice Manager or Lead GP so the patient can be informed honestly and promptly.
  • Risk Assessments:
    • Moving & Handling: Assess before lifting or moving heavy items—use correct technique or request assistance.
    • Infection Control: Perform personal risk assessments (e.g. PPE availability, sharps safety, cleaning protocols).
  • Daily Awareness:
    • Check notice boards and the EMIS Homepage for new protocols or safety updates on TeamsNet.
    • Report damaged equipment or hazards immediately.
    • Ensure personal safety when dealing with aggressive or distressed patients—use the Panic Button if needed.
    • Keep walkways clear and maintain a clean, organised reception area.
