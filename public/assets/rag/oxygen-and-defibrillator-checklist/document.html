THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
OXYGEN AND DEFIBRILLATOR CHECKLIST

Introduction

Your oxygen cylinder(s) and defibrillator should be checked on a weekly basis as a minimum. A daily check is recommended. A designated person within the practice should be chosen to do this. There should also be a deputy in case of holidays and sickness.

Responsibilities

Oxygen - Primary checking responsibility Practice Manager
Deputy is the Senior Receptionist

Defibrillator - Primary checking responsibility Practice Manager
Deputy is the Senior Receptionist

Where the person designated with primary responsibility is to be absent for five or more working days, a check should be carried out prior to departure, and specific instructions left with the deputy for scheduled checks in their absence.

In the event of an expected or unexpected absence, the deputy next "in line" must assume responsibility for the routine check schedule.

Basic Standards

Remember - oxygen is dangerous in high concentrations and strongly supports combustion.

Before checking cylinders, ensure your hands are clean. If moisturisers or hand creams have been used, ensure hands are dry.
If alcohol-based gels or liquids (i.e. some anti-bacterial hand sanitisers) have recently been used, ensure it has totally evaporated before handling the cylinder.
Check there is no hot equipment in the room (e.g. Cauterisers etc. left switched on).
Ensure that adequate stock levels are available and that a replacement policy is in place to replenish stock in a timely manner
