THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
Hand Washing and Hygiene Protocol

Hand washing helps to remove micro-organisms that might cause disease and is essential in the prevention of health care associated infections and the recontamination of equipment and materials.

Hand hygiene not only includes a rigorous guide to thorough hand washing but also the use of hand washes and disinfectant gels.

Hand hygiene should be included as part of the staff induction policy and training should be given by Nurse Mishalu Joseph our Cross Infection Control Lead.

Hand washing technique posters should also be displayed above each dedicated hand washing sink. Hand washing technique should be observed at all times and strictly adhered to.

Levels of Hygiene should be displayed in the staff room.

Please refer to this guideline so that you know which soap to use and in which circumstance and how long to wash your hands for.

Level 1 refers to social hand hygiene - using mild liquid soap for 15- 20 seconds
Level 2 refers to procedures that will destroy microorganisms- using 2-4% for Chlorohexidine 15-30 seconds
Level 3 refers to surgical scrubbing - using 2-4% Chlorohexidine Gluconate for 2-3 minutes.

Facilities
Separate hand washing basins should be provided from basins used for other purposes
Hand washing basins need to be fitted with a sensor operated or lever operated mixer tap.
Taps should be positioned away from the plug hole to prevent aerosols coming up from the drain pipe.
Liquid soap should be in wall mounted disposable containers.
Hand cream should be in wall mounted disposable containers.
Wall mounted paper towel dispensers should be provided with disposable paper towels.
Common cotton or similar towels must not be used as they facilitate transmission of infection
Disinfectant gel should be provided in wall mounted disposable containers.
Bins with a foot control or sensor should be provided so that disposable towels can be disposed of without touching the bin lids.

General Hygiene Principals
Rings, bracelets and watches should be removed
Nails should be short and clean
Nail varnish and false nails should NEVER be worn
Wedding bands are permitted but not advisable

Hand hygiene should be done:
Before and after each patient
Before and after removal of Personal Protective Equipment
Before touching sterilised instruments
After washing dirty instruments
After cleaning any decontamination equipment
After using the toilet
Before and after eating food
When hands are visibly soiled
After using a computer keyboard
After blowing, wiping or touching your nose.

Routine Hand Washing
Mild liquid soap should be provided for routine hand washing. (Level 1 hygiene). Bar soap is strictly banned!
Chlorohexidine liquid soap should be provided for Level 2 & 3 hand hygiene.
Hands should always be washed under running water to remove residue & prevent irritation.
Never use a nail brush as this may damage skin where microorganisms can harbour.
Check hands are visibly clean, if not repeat process.
Pat hands dry with paper towels to remove transient microorganisms and avoid damaging the skin.

Hand Washing Technique
Remove all jewellery - rings, bracelets and watches before washing hands.
Ensure that the nails are clipped short (do not wear artificial nails), roll the sleeves up to the elbow.
Wet the hands and wrists, keeping hands and wrists lower than the elbows. This enables the water to flow to the fingertips, avoiding contamination of the arms
Apply soap (plain or antimicrobial as the procedure dictates) and lather thoroughly.
Use firm, circular motions to wash the hands and arms up to the wrists. Cover all areas including palms, back of the hands, fingers, between fingers and lateral side of fifth finger, knuckles, and wrists. Rub for minimum of 10-15 seconds.
Repeat the process if the hands are very soiled.
Clean under the fingernails using a blunt stick.
Rinse hands thoroughly, keeping the hands lower than the forearms.
Do no dip your hands into a bowl to rinse, as this re contaminates them.
Dry hands thoroughly with disposable paper towel or napkins.

Discard the towel if used, in an appropriate container without touching the bin lids with hand.

Using Alcohol Gels
After washing and drying of hands, apply the product to the palm of one hand and rub hands together for 15-30 seconds, covering all surfaces of hands and fingers, until hands are dry.
Do not rinse.

Aftercare
Hand cream should be provided and it is recommended that it is used to prevent cracking and chapping of skin.
Hand cream should be in wall mounted disposable containers.

Facilities
We provide a dedicated sink for hand washing in each consulting and treatment room
A diagram of correct hand washing is displayed above the sink
Lever operator mixer taps are provided
Do not discharge water straight into the plug hole (aerosols)
Disposable hand washing dispensers should be provided.
