THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
PRACTICE SECURITY
Introduction

All members of staff have responsibility for the daytime security of the surgery. Those members of staff who are responsible for opening and closing the surgery should also adhere to the following procedures:

Opening the surgery:

Where possible, this should never be done on your own. The first person to arrive should wait at a discrete distance from the door. The second person to arrive should observe that the first person is not under duress or observation before approaching.
A minimum of two persons should be present before the building is approached and entered. One person should remain at a distance outside until the first person has entered, and returns to indicate that it is all clear.

During the day:

All records (computer and manual/hard copy files) must be kept safe and secure at all times; access must be reserved for authorised people.
Access to consulting rooms, treatment rooms etc. must be carefully monitored.
Implementing a clean-desk policy can help to ensure that sensitive documents are securely locked away.
If leaving your consulting room you must log out of EMIS and remove your smart card and lock the door.

Last thing at night or on closing the surgery:

A minimum of two persons should lock up together.
Make a thorough check of all rooms both upstairs and down (including the toilets).
Ensure nobody else is left in the building, all windows are shut and securely locked, all computers have been logged out and turned off, all lights have been turned off and all doors have been closed.
Ensure front door has been locked.
Turn off other machines e.g. photocopier.
Check any unnecessary lights are not left on.
Set the alarm following the prescribed protocol.

In the event of an emergency, if you are alone:

If you feel physically threatened and there is no one else in the surgery, immediately telephone the police, giving your location and saying you are on your own.
If you are alone in reception or other parts of the surgery but there are others in the building, telephone through to the nearest internal telephone to make others aware you are in danger. FAILING THIS CALL FOR HELP.
Activate the EMIS red button alarm 'panic' facility following the prescribed protocol. See also:
