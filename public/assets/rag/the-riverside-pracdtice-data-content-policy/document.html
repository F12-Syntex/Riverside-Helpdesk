Practice Scanning Procedure

All staff involved in scanning, log onto the Clinical system using their own user ID and password

DOCUMENTS/INFORMATION FLOW NOT SCANNED

All post marked ‘PERSONAL & CONFIDENTIAL’ goes directly into the relevant person’s pigeon-hole and is not opened.
Practice Manager’s mail is not opened.
All Financial Documents, including bank statements (unopened), invoices, cheques and receipts go to Dr Goel and in his absence the practice manager. All Patients’ Prescription Requests to be stapled accordingly and placed in Repeat Prescription box in Reception.
All clinical mail for doctors to be opened and scanned and sent via EMIS wokflow to the relevant clinician.
Non-clinical mail for people that no longer work here to be left un-opened and returned to sender with a note on the envelope indicating this.
Publicity type information is put in the pigeon hole for staff member responsible,
District Nurses and Health Visitors Mail is placed in their pigeon-hole.
All forms – Medical Attendance Reports, Claim Forms etc are to be processed by the clinical staff.
Any letters for patients that are not registered here or have been deducted are put into an envelope addressed to SBS at 3 Caxton Road, Fullwood, Preston, Lancs, PR2 9ZZ.
Email : . Tel: 03330142884
Breast Screening Programme results are passed to the GP to enter onto the patient’s notes and then scanned.
Any information that cannot be scanned is filed.
Change of Address information is passed to Admin Team member responsible.
Registration Forms and information are passed to Admin Team member responsible.
Capitation printouts go to the Senior GP (Dr Goel)

SCANNING
SCANNED DOCUMENTATION

Clinical Post and faxes are scanned.

Maternity Discharge letters and notices of abortion or miscarriages are scanned and also work-flowed via EMIS system are sent to Admin Team member responsible.

Cytology – Hysterectomy information and all cytology letters and results are scanned and results given to Admin Team member responsible.

Histopathology and Immunology results are all checked every day via links and filed into patient notes. If they are not on the clinical system they are scanned and then filed daily with the scanned correspondence.

All Discharge Letters and Palliative care patients correspondence are scanned – and Once a month is printed off and given to the Community Matron.

After scanning any Diabetic Retinopathy DNA letters are passed to the Admin Team member responsible for follow up.

Spirometry clinic results are scanned to the relevant GP and then passed to the Admin Team member responsible to scan.

All out of hours notes are saved in patients files.

Any letters for babies who are not yet registered are filed in ‘unnamed babies’ file which is checked regularly until fully registered.
All Accident and Emergency reports once scanned and sent to the clinician via EMIS workflow system.
All letters concerning children under 5 are scanned and a copy is put into the pigeon hole of the Health Visitor.
Screening Results are scanned plus BPs, height, weight and smoking status are entered on to the patient’s notes. Any scan images are filed in the patient’s Lloyd George notes.

Any consultant’s letters with reference to us taking up on–going care for any patient with a PSA result is scanned and then passed to the GP via EMIS workflow system.

ECGs, 24 BP Monitoring Reports and Spirometry Results – all these results of tests carried out at the practice are all scanned.

Undertake daily server backups to make sure data is safe.

Manage and monitor backup tapes to an agreed protocol to include the necessary validation of tapes.

Patient records are maintained for:

Legibility
That all records are complete and unchanged from the original
That documents are attached to the correct patient record
That scanned documents can be printed off onto paper for transfer with original Lloyd George envelope.

TO MAKE SURE THE DOCUMENTS SCANNED ARE RETRIEVABLE –1 IN 20 DOCUMENTS IS CHECKED TO ENSURE THAT IT IS AN ACCURATE COPY OF WHAT WAS SCANNED.

Protocol for Scanning
‘Encounter’ is set for Administration.
Printed list is organised to try to keep patient care continuity when covering absent GPs for the purpose of workflow.
Filing Documents
Letters are scanned making sure to capture any that are double sided.
There is then a choice of the Intellisense System or the Filing System to workflow the document to the GP and then file in the clinical system.
The Intellisense System is not used for handwritten documents.
The Intellisense System reads the documents and identifies the patient, clinic date; Hospital, Department and type of document which can then be adjusted if necessary and work flowed to the GP or filed directly into the patient’s record where you choose where you want the document filed.
The Filing System allows you to pick individually the correct matches for the document.
The Filing System and the Intellisense Systems are maintained by us adding or deducting different entries to allow easier identification.
The Intellisense System allows for certain Read Codes to be added and matched against eg Diabetic Retinopathy.
Diabetic Retinopathy results are scanned and results entered
The System is continually being monitored and maintained.

All documentation is filed on a daily basis and is kept in the filing Administration Room.

Retention of scanned documents
Scanned documents are retained for 3 months and then shredded, once scanning accuracy has been checked

Retention period of records
GP medical records are retained at the practice for the length of the patient registration or until patient deceased. Electronic patient records (EPRs) are not destroyed, or deleted, for the foreseeable future.

If the patient has left the practice/ moved out of the practice or after the event of death the medical records are then sent via courier to the Health Authority and are kept for 10 years after death or after the patient has permanently left the country unless the patient remains in the European Union. In the case of a child if the illness or death could have potential relevance to adult conditions or have genetic implications for the family of the deceased, the advice of clinicians should be sought as to whether to retain the records for a longer period.
