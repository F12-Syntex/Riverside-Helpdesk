THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
LOCUM APPOINTMENT PROTOCOL
INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this protocol is to provide a framework which will ensure that all prudent steps are taken prior to the appointment of a locum GP to ensure continued patient welfare and safety, and the compliance with statutory requirements. It is essential for practices to hold a comprehensive file containing all documentation and checks, to be retained indefinitely.

BACKGROUND
The Health and Social Care Act 2001 initiated changes to the NHS (General Medical Services) Regulations including Terms of Service. From April 2002 specific requirements were imposed on GP Practices to ensure the validity of non-principals (including locums) and these requirements are incorporated within the procedures given below, and the checklist at Appendix A should be completed for all non-principal GPs and retained indefinitely within the personal file. All checks should be complete prior to appointment / starting work.

SPECIFIC CHECKS
Initial Contact with the Locum
Ask for (preferably in writing):
Brief factual C.V.
GMC registration and defence registration detail or certificates
Copy of Licence to Practise
Details of PCO holding performer registration
Performer registration number (usually in the format nnnnnnn / nnn - usually the doctors GMC number followed by the PCO organisational code)
Details of last Criminal Records Bureau check (from April 2013 known as Disclosure & Barring Scheme)
Details of GP work over the last 3 years.
Names of 2 recent clinical referees
Knowledge of the practice clinical system (EMIS / System One)
Smartcard number & NHS email address

On receipt of the written information noted above, check the following:

GMC
Check registration with the GMC. A registration status check is also available on-line at
www.GMC-uk.org
or is available by telephone enquiry. Record and print the result. Obtain a copy of the Licence to Practise. Ensure that the GP does not have a restriction in place or that this has been declared. Where a restriction or interim order is in place print off the full details of the restriction / order and ensure compliance prior to engagement.

Defence Registration
Check that the documents produced are current and valid. Where only renewal letters are produced ensure that a confirmation of renewal is available, such as a certificate or a receipt.

Performers List
Check that the locum is included on a Primary Care Organisation (PCO) Medical Performer List. The Locum should be able to provide the name of the PCO and the personal registration number. Contact the PCO (preferably in writing) and ask for confirmation that the doctor is registered and that they are free from restrictions. Check that a Criminal Records Bureau Check as been performed and is available (suggested 2 year period).

Where a locum provides documentary evidence of their registration then this may accepted however this must not replace personal contact from the practice to the registering PCO for direct confirmation. Care should be taken as documents presented by locums may be out of date and may not record recent restrictions.

A doctor may only be on one PCO list, but this will enable him / her to work for alternative PCOs or their Practices. Locum doctors should be registered on their "local" list where most of their work is performed; however where a doctor moves geographically and "forgets" to transfer their registration then this is acceptable.

Where a doctor is registered on a PCO List a separate Criminal Records Bureau check is not required. Further information on the management of medical performers lists can be obtained from the Department of Health website

Primary Medical Performers Lists: Delivering Quality in Primary Care : The Department of Health - Pubs and stats: Publications

Referees
A minimum of 2 clinical referees are required. The locum may provide written references. Although these may be accepted they must not be relied on, and in all circumstances the practice should contact the referee in writing, and ask for confirmation and a clinical reference. The referees should normally be related to the last 2 clinical posts.

It is also good practice to telephone 2 of the practices at which the locum has worked in additional to the 2 clinical referees to determine their suitability.

Where a locum is engaged as a matter of urgency references may be taken up verbally pending written confirmation. This should be received within 14 days.

Agreement/contract
It is good practice to have a written agreement with a self-employed locum. Appendix B lists the key points which should be agreed, and there is a draft Locum Agreement in the HR index of Library [*].
If the locum is to be an employee, he/she should be treated in a similar manner to a salaried GP and given a contract of employment. If the locum is to be employed for a fixed period (e.g. for maternity cover), this must be specified in the contract. There is a draft contract of employment for a salaried GP in the HR index of the Library [*].

Use of Locum Agencies
If the Practice uses an agency who supplies locum doctors who change on a regular basis, then an undertaking should be obtained from the agency to the effect that only doctors currently included on a PCO Performers List, and who are unrestricted are provided.

Clinical System
In all cases locum GPs are to be provided with their own "sign-on" and are not to use a generic or other sign on (e.g. "Locum"). This is to ensure that the clinical system at all times will show the actual name of the doctor undertaking a consultation.
On arrival
Give tour of building showing fire exit, toilets common room and consulting room they are using. 

Ensure the locum is aware of use of computer system and advise locum of passwords.

Introduce locum to the reception staff, identify a contact if they have any problems and ensure they have telephone numbers for that contact and the Practice Manager.

Introduce locum to GPs and Practice staff.

Give locum pack consisting of:

Practice leaflet
How to use the surgery phone and internal extensions
External telephone numbers of significance
Prescribing formulary information
Copies of any Health Alerts issued within the last 12 months.
Panic alarm protocol
Details of services available locally including A&E, X ray
Details of referral
Procedures for review/contact with District Nurses
Procedures for checking lab test results
Surgery vaccine procedures
Other relevant surgery procedures
