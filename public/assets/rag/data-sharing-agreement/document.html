The Data Processor is the organisation (or person) that processes personal [and special category] data "on behalf of the data controller". The Data Processor can only act in response and instruction from the data controller, in accordance with the requirements of this Agreement. Any change to the processing arrangements or significant decisions about the use of the data must only be made with the prior written approval of the data controller.

Information Accuracy, Use, Retention and Deletion
The accuracy of the data shared with the Recipient will be the responsibility of the Discloser. The Discloser will therefore ensure that the data is accurate and up to date before the data is disclosed. If the Discloser becomes aware of any inaccuracies in shared data it has shared the Discloser should inform the other party(ies) immediately in order for the data to be corrected or recalled.

The Recipient must only use the information for the purposes set out in the Agreement above (see Purpose of Agreement). The Recipient must also ensure that the processing of the Information does not give rise to any inaccuracies. Any inaccuracies detected must be rectified to maintain data quality.

The Recipient must not share the information with any third party without the written consent of the Discloser and subject to entering into a separate Data Sharing Agreement, the terms of which must be approved by the Discloser.
The information will be retained by the Recipient for [set of the timescales that this information will be required for OR in accordance with Records Management Code of Practice for Health and Social Care schedules]. The Information will then be destroyed by the Recipient in a secure and confidential manner and the Recipient will notify the Discloser that this has been done.

FOIA Requests/Breaches/Complaints/Subject Access Requests

Subject Access Request

The Discloser and Recipient must have a Subject Access Request policy and procedure in place in order to satisfy a data subject's right to access their personal information under the Data Protection Act.

Freedom of Information Act Requests

The Discloser and Recipient acknowledge that their dealings in respect of this Agreement may become the subject of FOIA requests and recognise that the legal time limit for providing information is 20 working days. The Discloser and Recipient will therefore work together in dealing with any such requests by notifying the other party as soon as they receive a request.

Breaches

The Discloser and Recipient must report all breaches of confidentiality / information security in relation to this Agreement to the information governance leads of the Discloser and Recipient and keep them appraised of progress and the outcome of the investigation.

Examples of breaches include, but are not limited to:

Unauthorised disclosure of personal [and special category] data
Unauthorised access of personal [and special category] data
Inappropriate use of the data shared in relation to this Agreement
Data loss
Cyber security breach
Inadequate security arrangements resulting in a "near miss"

Each Party must maintain an Information Risk, Security and Incident Management Policy and Procedure and ensure that each breach in managed in accordance with that policy.

Complaints

Any complaints arising as a result of / or in connection with this Agreement must be reported to the information governance leads of the relevant party. The party in receipt of the complaint must deal with it in accordance with their complaints policy/procedure. The party in receipt of the complaint will keep the other party appraised of progress and the outcome.

In the event that more than one party is the subject of the complaint, all parties must agree a joint approach for handling the complaint, including arrangements and timescales.
