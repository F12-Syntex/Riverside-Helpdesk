Referral Protocol & Additional Guidance

At various times, patients will need to be referred for a consultant opinion, operation, or procedure, e.g. x-ray, MRI etc., to another service or to another clinician within the practice.

When this happens, the Referrer should ensure the following takes place and is recorded accurately in the patient record ( you may wish to include Read Codes for various entries so that you are able to undertake searches at a later date for audit purposes - templates within the clinical system can be devised and used to ensure consistency and accuracy).

Where abuse is suspected, the patient and / or their carer should be advised about safeguarding arrangements, how they work and what other agencies may be involved:

  • Discussion regarding the need to refer and positive / negative outcomes.
  • The choices that the patient has about where to go for treatment.
  • Where the patient indicates that they would prefer treatment or investigations carried out privately, explain there will be costs attached to this option (payable to the private provider) and that they will be responsible for these, but that no charge will be levied by the Practice for writing the referral;
  • Patient’s preference on where to go.
  • How Choice and Booking works.
  • Whether the patient agrees with the management plan.
  • What the timescale is likely to be from referral to appointment.
  • Ask if the patient has any specific needs that the service being referred to needs to be aware of, e.g. carer, social issues, disability, advance directives etc.
  • If the patient is a military veteran and the problem is directly connected to their service, ensure this is noted in the referral (see Military Veterans - Protocol for Priority Treatment).
  • Where patients require diagnostic tests, including imaging, explain what they are and why they are needed together with how they should access the results.
  • Test that the patient has understood what they have been told and invite questions.
  • Allow the patient time to reflect on what has been discussed and if they choose not to go ahead with the referral, respect their decision.
  • Take care when dictating referrals and do this as soon as possible after the consultation, ideally within two working days from the appointment.
  • Letters should be checked for accuracy.
  • Include patient name, address, date of birth, NHS number, telephone number, past medical history, current medication together with any allergies or sensitivities, details of any investigations or tests that have been undertaken including the results.
  • Include information if the patient poses a risk to themselves or others, e.g. infection, suicide, temperament etc.
  • If a child or young person being referred for specialist healthcare is suspected to be at risk of, or suffering, abuse, or neglect, you should make sure that you include relevant information about the child protection concerns in the referral.
  • If the referral is delayed for any reason, the patient should be notified asap.
  • The referring clinician will be responsible for the continuing care of the patient and will act as the point of contact if there are any issues arising out of the refer
  • Please use the dedicated CCEG documents in Emis to refer or Dr Goel’s template letter for general referrals (See FY2 induction information). Remember to call the document the correct name, eg “Homerton consultant and specialty” and code “refer to specialty” for audit purposes.

2-week referrals

  • If making a 2-week referral always use the specific 2-week referral forms in EMIS. Always check that the symptoms meet the 2-week criteria
  • Complete the referral letter within 24 hours and ideally the same day.
  • Send an urgent task to the reception group, who will book the appointment on ERS.
  • Always confirm with the patient the 2-week referral and your concerns and that the appointment may be by phone.
  • Ask the patient to contact the surgery in 2 days if no appointment has been received from the hospital.
  • To safety net referrals, the referral document is coded automatically, and reception should also add the referral on the dedicated 2 week admin template with a diary date of 2 weeks.
  • Dr Goel runs the CEG 2 week search 2 weekly to track referrals and informs the practice manager if any actions are identified.
  • In his absence the practice manager runs this search and actions.
