THE RIVERSIDE PRACTICE
PATIENT DATA (RECORDING OF) POLICY

Introduction

The following document sets out the practice policy on recording patient contacts.
Policy

All patient data is recorded on the practice computer system. The policy of the practice is that users will not duplicate patient information within paper records.

The advantages of recording patient information digitally include:

Entries are automatically dated and timed and the system generates an audit trail.
The identity of the clinician or administrator is automatically recorded.
Practice-defined read code sets allow for uniform data entry and retrieval.
Typed or coded text is always legible.
Data entered is immediately accessible to all (authorised) users.

It is the policy of the practice that any patient contact with either a clinician or an administrator, including telephone contacts or general enquiries, is recorded in the patient's record on the clinical computer system, and that:

Every patient health encounter must be recorded in the system.
All of the patient's medications must be listed in the patient's record.
All records, hospital letters and investigation reports are available electronically in date order.
Minimum Requirements for Data Entry

The minimum data entry requirement for any encounter is:

The user must be logged into the secure system using their unique username and password.
The entry must be assigned to the correct patient - mistakes can happen, so always check.
The location (surgery, home, telephone, DNA, failed or indirect encounter if recording non-contact event) must be recorded.
The entry must include the date and time (this is system generated).
All entries must be via a suitable SNOMED / Read Code. The minimum amount of information acceptable is a paragraph of free text summarising the nature of the contact, however the incidence of the use of free text must be minimal, and should only be used where an appropriate SNOMED / Read Code is not available. Clinical data entered as free text is difficult to retrieve.
LOCUMS AND ATTACHED STAFF

The Practice requires that locums and attached staff also enter data resulting from any patient contact that takes place within the surgery via the clinical system. Temporary clinical staff will be employed on the basis that they have specific clinical system on EMIS WEB experience, and that they have a significant degree of familiarity with the requirements of QOF and data entry.

All temporary system users or attached staff system users must be assigned their own personal
log-in. Generic log-in credentials (e.g. "Midwife" or "Locum") are not to be used.

Practice policy is that all users receive the following basic training before their first session:

A thorough understanding of system security, including logging on using the unique user identity allocated by the system administrator.
How to 'call-in' patients who have arrived for booked appointments.
How to search for patients in the electronic patient directory.
Correct recording of types of contact, e.g.
Face to face consultation
Telephone consultation.
Email consultation.
Consultation via Video Conference
Home Visit note
Administration note
DNAs.
All failed contacts must also be recorded.
Recording of lifestyle information (e.g. height, weight, BP, smoking).
Using practice-defined clinic screens, where appropriate.
Using practice-defined read code lists, where appropriate.
Accessing scanned letters and electronic test results.
Appropriate (and avoiding inappropriate) use of free text.
Prescribing (GP locums only).
Clearing patient records from the screen.
Logging off.
Printer controls and paper trays.
Who to contact for help.

The system administrator routinely audits data entry, and will be able to offer additional training and support where needed.

Users new to the practice will be subject to sampling of data entry immediately after their first session and at intervals thereafter until it is clear that a satisfactory standard, in accordance with practice requirements, has been achieved.
