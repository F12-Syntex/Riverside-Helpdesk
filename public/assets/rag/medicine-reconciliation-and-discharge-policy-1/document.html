Medicine Reconciliation & Discharge Policy

Following a patient's stay in hospital, a visit to the A&E or a consultant it is possible that a patients current medication will have been changed. This could be by way of stopping a medication, starting a new one, or altering the dose of an existing medication.

Therefore the surgery should have a system in place to ensure that changes are made as soon as possible.

The surgery will receive notification either by the patient handing in a discharge note of the medication, or a hospital letter posted, or sent down the electronic link directly to the surgery.

These notifications should be added to the EMIS document wokflow and if needed scanned and sent to the on call doctor for them to review and make the alterations. The doctor can task the practice pharmacist to do a medicines reconciliation and update the patient records and liaise with the nominated pharmacy if needed.

Neither reception staff nor admin should make these changes due to the clinical nature.

The doctors or practice pharmacists will then either make those changes or issue a prescription if needed or if the letter is unclear, or confusing then a query must be raised to the issuing dept and until resolved, the medication should not be issued.

Usually a patient is given seven days' worth of medication following a stay in hospital that should give plenty of time to receive and act on any changes. This however does not always occur and the patient may request the items from the practice.

If the patient advises the surgery that they have been told of a medication change and the surgery has not received any paperwork then the surgery should follow it up with the outside dept and should not issue anything until they are satisfied that they have received all the information.

Clinicians should be satisfied that the forms received by the patient are genuine and have not been changed. This is very unlikely but if in any doubt then checks should be made.
