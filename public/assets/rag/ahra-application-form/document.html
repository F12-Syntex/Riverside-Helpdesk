Please read the following information carefully 

The Access to Health Records Act 1990 (AHRA) grants rights to certain individuals to see what has been recorded about a deceased patient.  This only applies however to written health records made on or after 1 November 1991. 

 

Access is available to: 

  • The patient’s personal representative (this will be the executor of the will or the administrator of the estate).
  • An individual who may have a claim arising out of the patient’s death.  
  • A third-party representative acting on behalf of the personal representative or a claimant. To make an application on behalf of the deceased patient’s personal representative, or a claimant, you must also complete the Letter of Authority form.

Privacy notice

Please take the time to read the information below as it explains how we will process your personal data

This privacy notice is for applicants making an access request for a deceased patient’s health records under the Access to Health Records Act 1990.

The GP practice will collect your personal data to process your application, and it will be stored securely in line with the practice’s records retention policy.

Your personal data will not be shared with third parties unless it is required for the prevention and detection of crime, or court proceedings.
